Summary

This is not a research paper, but a project announcement explaining that the website directory project 512kb Club is no longer accepting new submissions due to the maintenance burden and is seeking a trusted person to take it over. The post also notes in an update that the project was ultimately taken over by Brad Taunt.

Problem

  • The issue being addressed is not a scientific research problem, but the ongoing maintenance and ownership handoff of a community website directory project.
  • The author says that after running the project for about 5.5 years, the workload of reviewing new submissions, cleaning up old sites, updating listings, and related tasks had become too great and was no longer worth the time required.
  • This matters because without someone to take over, the project would stop being updated and could eventually go offline, affecting the visibility and continuity of the community that relies on the directory.

Approach

  • The core mechanism is very simple: stop accepting new submissions and find a trusted person to take over responsibility for the project's domain, code repository, hosting, and maintenance.
  • The author first announces that no new submissions will be accepted, while still working through the 25 open PRs at the time, after which the submission process will be closed.
  • If someone takes over, they must handle renewal of the 512kb.club domain, management of the GitHub repository, hosting, and day-to-day maintenance, while keeping the project in its current form.
  • If no one takes over, the author plans to make one final export of the Jekyll source site and convert it into a static site to preserve it until the domain is no longer renewed in the future.
  • The update states that Brad Taunt ultimately took over, completing the continuity plan for the project.

Results

  • The post does not provide scientific experiments, model metrics, or benchmark results, so there are no quantitative research results to report.
  • The most specific project-status numbers mentioned are that the project has been running for about 5.5 years, has received nearly 2,000 pull requests over time, and currently lists about 950 websites.
  • At the time of the announcement, there were still 25 open PRs waiting to be processed, after which new submissions would stop.
  • The final outcome-related development is that, in an update on 2026-03-08, the author confirmed that Brad Taunt had agreed to take over 512kb Club, and the domain transfer was underway.
