Summary

This article introduces the Verge TS Pro/TS Ultra electric motorcycles, whose core selling point is replacing the traditional in-frame motor with a reverse electromagnetic structure where “the rear wheel is the motor.” Its significance lies in freeing up battery packaging space, simplifying the drivetrain and thermal management system, and delivering differentiated performance through extremely high torque and relatively low curb weight.

Problem

  • Traditional motorcycles, as well as most electric motorcycles, still use the old architecture of an “in-frame motor + chain/shaft drive,” which limits battery space and overall vehicle packaging.
  • Placing the motor close to the battery creates issues in thermal management, cooling-system complexity, weight, and cost, and may raise the center of gravity, affecting low-speed handling.
  • Conventional hub motors may become too heavy in ultra-high-power/torque scenarios, making them less suitable for high-performance electric motorcycle design.

Approach

  • Verge makes the drive motor into the rear wheel itself, rather than placing the motor inside the frame and then driving the rear wheel through a chain or shaft.
  • The mechanism can be simply understood as “inverting” a traditional motor: the stationary part is on the inside, the rotating part is on the outside, and the outer ring rotates directly as the wheel.
  • This allows more of the 20.8 kWh battery to be packaged in the lower half of the frame, freeing up central space and lowering the center of gravity.
  • By eliminating the chain/driveshaft and claiming no separate motor cooling system is needed, it can reduce components, weight, cost, and mechanical complexity.
  • Torque acts directly on the wheel and output can be controlled on a millisecond scale, improving response speed.

Results

  • The TS Pro is claimed to produce 137 hp and 738 lb-ft of torque; the TS Ultra is claimed to produce 201 hp and 885 lb-ft of torque.
  • The TS Pro is claimed to do 0-60 mph in 3.5 seconds, have a range of 217 miles, and support Level 3 fast charging.
  • Verge claims a curb weight of about 450 pounds; the article compares this with the Energica Ego+ at 573 pounds / 171 hp / 164 lb-ft, the LiveWire One at 562 pounds / 100 hp / 86 lb-ft, and the S2 Mulholland at 432 pounds / 84 hp / 194 lb-ft.
  • On pricing, the TS Pro starts at $30,000 and the TS Ultra starts at $45,000; deliveries could begin as early as the end of the year.
  • The article’s strongest conclusion is that this design is highly novel in power, torque, weight, and packaging, but the riding experience is still affected by suspension tuning, handlebar/footpeg ergonomics, and the handling feel of the large rear tire.
