Summary

MoatRadar is an AI product for investment research rather than a traditional academic paper; it claims to analyze stocks, startups, and crypto assets in about 30 seconds using 40+ investment frameworks from Buffett, Graham, Damodaran, and others. Its overall value proposition is to package the research methods of multiple well-known investors into a searchable, scalable automated research tool.

Problem

  • Investment research is usually time-consuming and limited in coverage, and different asset classes (stocks, startups, crypto) often require different analysis workflows.
  • Ordinary investors find it difficult to systematically apply mature investment principles like Buffett’s to scan global market opportunities, which affects research efficiency and consistency.
  • With 40,000+ stocks, 70+ exchanges, and multiple investment frameworks, the cost of manual screening is high, creating a need for faster automated research tools.

Approach

  • The core mechanism is simple: the user inputs a research target or search request, the system calls GPT-4o and combines it with Financial Modeling Prep data to generate research results based on famous investors’ frameworks in about 30 seconds.
  • The product abstracts multiple well-known methods into 80+ investor frameworks, applied uniformly across three asset classes: stocks, startups, crypto.
  • It identifies candidate opportunities through large-scale market scanning, with the marketing claim that a single search can return “up to 20 opportunities.”
  • In essence, this is a research automation interface of “LLM + financial data source + predefined investment frameworks,” rather than a newly described algorithm in detail.

Results

  • The clearest result stated in the text is speed: Results in 30 seconds, meaning research results are produced in about 30 seconds.
  • Claimed coverage scale: 40,000+ stocks, covering 70+ global exchanges.
  • Claimed method library scale: supports principles from 40+ investors, as well as 80+ investor frameworks.
  • Claimed search output: 1 search = €1 = up to 20 opportunities.
  • However, the provided content does not include standard academic evaluations, public datasets, accuracy, returns, backtests, or quantitative comparisons with baseline methods, so its research quality or investment performance improvements cannot be verified.
