Summary

This is not a research paper, but a product post introducing a language-learning tool that automatically transcribes podcast links into text. It is designed to help learners improve comprehension and persistence in learning by checking transcripts when they cannot clearly hear connected speech or miss parts of the content.

Problem

  • Language learners often struggle to understand foreign-language podcasts because of fast speech, heavy connected speech, and words blending together.
  • Repeatedly pausing, replaying, and manually looking up translations significantly interrupts the learning flow, reduces motivation, and can even lead to giving up.
  • There is a lack of a convenient tool tailored to podcast listening scenarios that can directly turn episode audio into readable text.

Approach

  • The author built PodcastsToText, where users can directly paste individual episode links from Spotify or Apple Podcasts.
  • The system automatically transcribes podcast content into text, allowing users to review the full transcript when they do not understand something instead of constantly replaying it manually.
  • The product uses a freemium model, with a free allowance of up to 30 minutes of transcription.
  • The core mechanism can be simply understood as: input a podcast link, automatically generate a transcript, and support listening comprehension in language learning.

Results

  • The text does not provide formal experiments, benchmark data, or paper-style quantitative results.
  • The most specific product capabilities described are support for Spotify and Apple Podcasts link input, with automatic transcription.
  • The explicit limitation given is that the free tier supports transcription of up to 30 minutes of content.
  • The author’s central claim is that the tool helps learners “worry less about catching every single word” and instead improve comprehension, review, and language internalization through transcripts, but no quantitative comparisons or user-study data are provided."
