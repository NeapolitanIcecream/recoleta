Summary

This study compared subjective responses to real photographs of nude women and various artificially generated/stylized nude images, finding that although AI-generated female nude images were rated as less “real” than actual photographs, they ranked highest in aesthetic pleasure, sexual attractiveness, and overall pleasantness. This suggests that digitally generated content may be reshaping people’s standards for beauty and sexual appeal.

Problem

  • The study aimed to address how people subjectively evaluate “real” versus “artificial” images of the female nude in today’s environment filled with AI, CGI, cosmetic-enhanced imagery, sex dolls, and hentai.
  • This matters because sexual stimulus materials used in past psychology research may no longer be suitable for younger generations, while digital media is changing how humans perceive attractiveness, authenticity, and pleasantness.
  • The research also sought to clarify whether different age and gender groups show systematic differences in preference across these image categories.

Approach

  • Researchers conducted a nationwide online survey in the Czech Republic, recruiting 649 people who self-identified as sexually attracted to women, the majority of whom were men, along with 45 women.
  • Participants viewed 6 categories of static images of nude women: real photographs, computer-generated images, AI-generated images, cosmetically enhanced women, silicone sex dolls, and hentai illustrations.
  • Each category included 5 matched character types and body type/hair color combinations. The research team also standardized the images, such as by removing tattoos and jewelry and unifying skin tone, to control confounding variables as much as possible.
  • Participants rated each image on 0–100 sliders for realism, sexual attractiveness, and aesthetic attractiveness, and used a 5-point pictorial scale to rate emotional pleasantness (valence); image order was randomized.
  • The core mechanism was simple: have the same group of people directly compare different image types on subjective rating differences in “how real they look” and “whether they are more attractive/more pleasant.”

Results

  • The sample size was 649; the study compared 6 image categories, each with 5 character variants.
  • For realism, real photographs ranked #1 and AI-generated images ranked #2; they were followed by computer-generated, cosmetically enhanced, sex doll, and hentai images.
  • For aesthetic attractiveness, sexual attractiveness, and emotional pleasantness, AI-generated images ranked #1 and real photographs ranked #2; the passage did not provide specific means, effect sizes, or significance values.
  • Images of cosmetically enhanced women ranked lowest in aesthetic attractiveness and overall pleasantness; hentai and sex doll images were also rated relatively low overall and were often judged as somewhat unpleasant.
  • Regarding gender differences, men gave higher overall ratings than women across all categories for realism, attractiveness, aesthetics, and pleasantness; however, women showed a similar category ranking pattern to men.
  • Regarding age differences, older participants gave higher aesthetic and sexual attractiveness ratings to real photographs, AI images, and CG images; younger participants showed a clear preference for hentai. The text did not provide specific statistical values for these differences."
