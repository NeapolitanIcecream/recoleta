ISTQB Certifications Under the Lens: Their Contributions to the Software-Testing Profession; and AI-assisted Synthesis of Practitioners' Endorsements and Criticisms

Summary

This paper systematically examines the value and controversies of ISTQB certifications for the software testing profession, and uses an AI-assisted multivocal literature review combined with expert validation to synthesize practitioners' support and criticisms. The conclusion is: ISTQB has clear value in professional recognition and establishing a common language, but its coverage of practical skills and its fit with industry needs remain subjects of ongoing debate.

Problem

  • The problem this study addresses is: what real value have the globally widespread ISTQB certifications brought to the software testing profession, and what limitations have practitioners continued to criticize? This matters because ISTQB has issued more than 1.2 million certifications and organized more than 1.4 million exams across more than 130 countries, deeply influencing hiring, training, and career development.
  • Existing discussions are often polarized: supporters argue that it provides a standardized body of knowledge and a professional entry threshold, while critics argue that it is too theoretical, too memory-based, and cannot represent real testing ability.
  • The study also addresses a methodological question: can GenAI support systematic evidence synthesis under strict human supervision, for summarizing practitioner perspectives from both academic and grey literature.

Approach

  • The authors conducted an AI-assisted Multivocal Literature Review (MLR), integrating both academic literature and grey literature to answer two core questions: how practitioners endorse ISTQB, and how they criticize it.
  • The core mechanism is straightforward: ChatGPT’s “deep research” capability was used to help search, screen, organize, and synthesize a large volume of practitioner opinions, while maintaining continuous human oversight throughout the process, along with quality assurance, explainability, and observability measures to reduce the risk of AI-induced review distortion.
  • The study did not stop at “what the AI summarized”; it also introduced 4 independent experts to evaluate whether the supporting views were “precise” and whether the criticisms were “fair.”
  • The paper also provides an integrative overview of the ISTQB certification system, including its 23 certifications, layered curriculum structure, contributions to the software testing body of knowledge (STBoK), and the evolution of its coverage of topics such as automation, AI, security, and performance.

Results

  • The main supporting views reported in the paper include: career development and job opportunities, professional recognition and credibility, and shared terminology/common language. The authors regard these as ISTQB’s most stable and most widely recognized contributions.
  • The main criticisms focus on: being overly theoretical, insufficient relevance to agile and automation-intensive environments, inability to effectively measure real testing skills, and overreliance on memorization.
  • For quantitative background data, the paper explicitly states that as of May 2025, ISTQB had reached 130+ countries, organized 1.4M+ exams, and issued 1.2M+ certifications; the certification portfolio covers 23 certifications, and it also notes 22+ syllabi covering topics such as automation, AI, security, game testing, and performance engineering.
  • The qualitative conclusion from the expert validation is that many supporting views are precise, and several criticisms are also fair to some extent, but they often reflect deeper tensions within the software testing field involving “school-of-thought differences” and “context dependence.”
  • The provided excerpt does not give more fine-grained quantitative evaluation results (for example, number of included studies, mean expert scores, significance tests, or numerical comparisons with baseline methods), so more specific performance metrics or statistical improvement magnitudes cannot be reported.
  • At the methodological level, the strongest concrete claim is: AI-assisted MLR can be used for systematic evidence synthesis under strict human supervision, and the authors fully document the execution logs and two rounds of iteration in the appendix to improve transparency and reproducibility.
