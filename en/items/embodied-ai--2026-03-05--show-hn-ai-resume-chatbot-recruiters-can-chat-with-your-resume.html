Summary

This is an AI resume platform for recruiting scenarios: after candidates upload their resumes, recruiters can discover them through semantic search, and they can optionally add a live AI chatbot to answer questions. Its core value is turning a static PDF resume into a searchable, conversational candidate profile page.

Problem

  • Traditional PDF resumes are static, easy to forget, and make it difficult for recruiters to quickly obtain additional information.
  • Keyword search can easily miss suitable candidates because the same experience is often described in different ways.
  • Recruiting teams also need to share candidate pools, collect resumes from external candidates, and let candidates control visibility.

Approach

  • Candidates upload PDF resumes, and the system automatically extracts work experience, skills, and education information to generate structured, searchable profiles.
  • Recruiters describe their needs in natural language, and the system matches by semantics rather than keywords, for example matching "scaled teams" to "grew org from 3 to 40".
  • The platform supports both public discovery and private organizational talent pools: candidates can make resumes public or share them only with specific organizations, and recruiting teams can collect resumes through dedicated links.
  • A paid feature provides a "live AI resume chatbot," allowing recruiters to ask questions directly about a candidate's background and receive instant answers, after which the candidate can be notified and join the conversation.

Results

  • The text does not provide paper-style experiments, benchmark data, or quantitative metrics, so there are no verifiable accuracy, recall, or conversion results.
  • The strongest specific product claim is that candidates can create a free profile in 2 minutes.
  • In pricing, the premium chatbot feature is offered at $19.99/month after an 8-month discount, or $159.99/year after a 1-year discount.
  • Functional claims include that recruiters can "search by meaning rather than keywords," teams can share candidate pools, avoid duplicate sourcing, and chat instantly with a candidate's AI, but the text provides no quantitative comparison.
