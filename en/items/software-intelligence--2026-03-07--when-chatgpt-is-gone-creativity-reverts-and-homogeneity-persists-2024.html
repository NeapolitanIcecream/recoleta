Summary

This paper investigates whether ChatGPT's enhancement of human creativity can be sustained. The conclusion is: ChatGPT can temporarily improve creative performance while it is being used, but once it is removed, that improvement falls back, while content homogenization continues to persist.

Problem

  • The paper addresses the question of whether ChatGPT's enhancement of creativity is a sustainable improvement in ability or merely a short-term external boost that exists only while the tool is available.
  • This matters because if generative AI only provides temporary efficiency gains while making outputs increasingly similar, then long-term use may weaken human originality and diversity.
  • The authors also specifically examine a key question: after ChatGPT becomes unavailable, can human creativity retain the earlier gains, or does it return to the original baseline?

Approach

  • The authors conducted a pre-registered 7-day experiment and a follow-up survey 30 days after the experiment ended to observe the effects of ChatGPT's “presence” and “absence” on sustained creativity.
  • The data came from creative tasks completed by 61 college students, producing 3302 creative ideas and 427 creative solutions.
  • The treatment group could use ChatGPT during creative tasks, while the control group completed the tasks entirely on their own.
  • The study focused on two dimensions: first, whether creative performance continued to improve; second, whether content became increasingly homogenized.

Results

  • Over a 5-day creative process, ChatGPT's “boosting effect” remained consistently observable, meaning the treatment group showed higher creative performance than the condition without the tool.
  • However, when ChatGPT was unavailable on day 7, and in the 30-day follow-up after the experiment ended, this improvement reverted to baseline; the abstract does not provide specific scores or effect sizes.
  • The dataset included 61 participants, 3302 creative ideas, and 427 creative solutions, supporting its conclusion about short-term gains and long-term reversion.
  • More importantly, the study found that using ChatGPT caused content to become increasingly homogenized, and this homogenization effect persisted even after ChatGPT was absent.
  • The paper therefore argues that generative AI brings a temporary increase in creativity, but in the long run may “box in” human creative ability within a more similar expressive space.
  • The abstract does not provide specific quantitative metrics, significance levels, or precise numerical comparisons against strong baselines; the strongest quantitative information is mainly the experiment duration, participant count, and sample size.
