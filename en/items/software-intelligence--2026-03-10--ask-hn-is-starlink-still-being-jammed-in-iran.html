Summary

This is not a research paper, but a Hacker News question thread discussing whether Starlink is still being jammed in Iran. The content mainly consists of user speculation and brief comments, without providing verifiable research methods or evidence.

Problem

  • The question under discussion is: in the context of internet restrictions in Iran, are Starlink or other satellite internet services still being jammed?
  • This question matters because it relates to communication availability, information flow, and censorship resistance in restricted regions.
  • However, the thread itself provides no systematic data, experimental design, or authoritative sources, so no reliable conclusion can be drawn.

Approach

  • The content does not use a formal research method, but instead raises a current-status question in a community Q&A format.
  • The replies are mainly based on personal speculation, for example: "if it were completely jammed, no videos would be coming out" and "they may be trying to jam it, but it is hard to cover the entire country."
  • No measurement plan, technical analysis, comparative experiments, time-series data, or evidence from Starlink/regulators/independent monitoring organizations is provided.

Results

  • There are no quantitative results: no data is reported on coverage, throughput, latency, disconnection rate, jamming intensity, geographic distribution, or time range.
  • The strongest specific claim is only speculation from a comment: Iran "may" be unable to successfully jam Starlink across its entire landmass.
  • Another speculative claim is that if Starlink were effectively and comprehensively jammed, "no videos would be getting out."
  • Due to the lack of experiments, samples, and sources, these claims cannot be treated as research conclusions or breakthrough results.
