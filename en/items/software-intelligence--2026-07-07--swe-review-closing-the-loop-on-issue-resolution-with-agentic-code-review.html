Summary

SWE-Review adds an agentic code-review step after AI-generated pull requests, so a coding agent can accept correct patches and revise failed ones. It introduces SWE-Review-Bench and SWE-Review-Traj to measure review decisions, review-guided revision, and training effects.

Problem

  • One-shot coding agents submit PRs without a reliable check that the issue is fixed.
  • Diff-only review can miss repository-level failures, such as symptom fixes that leave the root cause in another file.
  • The field lacks public benchmarks and training traces that test whether review feedback improves the final patch.

Approach

  • The input is a repository checkout, an issue, and an AI-generated PR; the reviewer can search files, inspect code, and run commands before it writes a review.
  • The reviewer outputs a binary decision, approve or request changes, plus a diagnosis with concrete defects and fix guidance.
  • SWE-Review-Bench contains 1,384 candidate PRs from 500 SWE-bench Verified issues, generated by GLM-5, Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B, and Qwen3-30B-A3B.
  • The paper evaluates Completion Rate, Decision Accuracy, and Resolve Rate after Revision, where rejected PRs are sent back to the generator with review feedback.
  • SWE-Review-Traj contains 8,914 decision-correct agentic review trajectories for training open reviewers.

Results

  • Iterative generate-review-revise raises resolve rate on SWE-bench Verified from 27.5% to 56.9% for Qwen3-30B-A3B, 50.9% to 68.8% for Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B, and 72.2% to 75.4% for GLM-5.
  • With Claude Opus 4.6 as reviewer, one-pass agentic review reaches RRR of 75.2% on GLM-5 PRs, 67.3% on Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B PRs, and 52.6% on Qwen3-30B-A3B PRs, compared with no-review baselines of 72.2%, 50.9%, and 27.5%.
  • On Qwen3-30B-A3B PRs, agentic review improves RRR over the best single-turn setting from 44.1% to 52.6%.
  • Training Qwen3-8B on SWE-Review-Traj raises completion rate from about 4% to 71.1-84.2% and decision accuracy from about 49-51% to 66.9-71.6% across PR-generator splits.
  • Diagnosis feedback improves revision in a 100-instance validation sample: no review scores 3% RRR, decision-only review scores 8%, teacher diagnosis scores 21%, and oracle review scores 32%.
  • Test-time review-guided revision raises resolve rate from 22.9% to 38.4% over 4 revision rounds.
