Summary

This article introduces the IP360 program launched by Japan’s Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI), which notably allows indie game developers to apply as individuals or unincorporated entities for grants of up to 10 million yen to support new IP development and overseas promotion. Its core significance lies in lowering the threshold for funding and thereby boosting the international competitiveness of Japan’s content industries, especially indie games.

Problem

  • The Japanese government wants to increase overseas revenue and global competitiveness for its domestic content industries, such as games and anime.
  • Traditional government funding typically requires applicants to be registered companies, making it difficult for many indie developers and individual creators to obtain support.
  • Indie teams often lack sufficient funding and institutional support for developing new original IP, international distribution, promotion, and localization.

Approach

  • METI launched the IP360 program, offering multiple funding channels for new IP creation and international expansion.
  • Among them, “New IP Creation Support (Startup Support)” allows individuals and unregistered entities to apply, which is especially important for indie developers.
  • The funding covers multiple stages, from pre-production and full production to post-production, game localization, and promotion.
  • Applicants must submit a prototype and business plan that explicitly includes an overseas distribution plan; only original new IP qualifies, while sequels, remakes, and other existing-IP projects are not eligible.
  • The government has explicitly stated that it will not interfere with the creative content or creative direction of funded works.

Results

  • A single project can receive up to 10 million yen in funding, approximately $62,000 USD.
  • The subsidy rate can cover up to 50% of eligible costs.
  • Funds can be used for pre-production, production, post-production, Localization, localization, promotion, as well as commissioning art/music, travel to overseas events, and communication expenses.
  • The Japanese government has set a goal of increasing annual overseas revenue related to Japanese content to 20 trillion yen (about $125.5 billion USD), and this goal was elevated to official national policy in 2025.
  • The article does not provide quantified outcome data showing results already produced by the grant program; the strongest concrete claim is that it significantly relaxes eligibility requirements for the first time, allowing individual and non-incorporated indie developers to enter the government funding system.
