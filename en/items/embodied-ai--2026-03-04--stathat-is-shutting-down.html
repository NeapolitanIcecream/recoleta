Summary

This is not a research paper, but a product shutdown notice: StatHat announced that it will shut down its service on April 4, 2026, and asked users to export their data before then. The core information is the service termination timeline and the data migration window, rather than any new technical method or experimental result.

Problem

  • Existing users need to be clearly informed that the service is about to stop, and why they must act quickly: otherwise, they may lose access to historical data after the shutdown.
  • An actionable data export plan is needed to reduce data loss and migration risks caused by the service closure.
  • This information is important, but it addresses a product operations/user communication issue rather than a technical problem in academic research or in robotics/foundation models.

Approach

  • Users were notified directly via email that StatHat will officially shut down in 30 days, on April 4, 2026.
  • A data export channel is provided before shutdown, along with an export instructions link: https://www.stathat.com/manual/export.
  • Key dates are explicitly listed to reduce user confusion:
    • Data export available until April 3, 2026
    • Service shuts down on April 4, 2026
  • The contact email contact@stathat.com is provided as a follow-up support channel.

Results

  • No research experiments, models, datasets, baselines, or quantitative metrics are provided, so there are no quantitative results to report.
  • The strongest concrete conclusion is the operational timeline: users have an approximately 30-day window to export their data.
  • The explicitly stated dates are: export deadline 2026-04-03, shutdown date 2026-04-04.
  • The text also notes that “there does not appear to be an announcement on the website yet,” indicating that the current public information may be reaching users mainly via email rather than through an official on-site page.
