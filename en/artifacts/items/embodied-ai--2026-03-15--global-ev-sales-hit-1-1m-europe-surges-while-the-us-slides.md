# Global EV sales hit 1.1M – Europe surges while the US slides

## Summary
This article summarizes the latest regional divergence in global EV sales in February 2026: the global total weakened, but Europe and some other markets grew strongly under subsidy support, while North America and China declined due to policy headwinds and policy adjustments, respectively. The core message is that the EV transition has not stalled overall; instead, it is increasingly being driven by regional policies, incentives, and trade rules.

## Problem
- The question the article seeks to explain is: **why global EV sales are showing clear regional divergence even as the overall total declines**.
- This matters because EV adoption is no longer determined only by technology or long-term trends; **policy incentives, taxes, trade, and subsidies** are now directly reshaping demand, supply chains, and export patterns.
- For industry and investors, regional divergence means **growth opportunities, supply chain risks, and market share shifts** are being rapidly reordered.

## Approach
- The article uses February 2026 sales data from **Benchmark Mineral Intelligence** to compare EV performance across **the world, China, Europe, North America, and other markets**.
- The core mechanism can be understood simply as: **where subsidies are restored or introduced, sales are stronger; where support is withdrawn or taxes rise, sales are weaker**.
- It further explains the differences through policy changes in various countries, such as new subsidies in Germany and Italy, weakening support policies in the US, China’s reinstated purchase tax, Canada’s adjustments to import tariff arrangements, and South Korea’s new subsidy program.
- Beyond sales figures, the article also uses automaker sales, export data, and supply-chain events such as layoffs to show how policy changes are transmitted across the broader industrial chain.

## Results
- **Global**: EV sales in the first two months of 2026 reached **2.2 million units**, **-8%** year over year; February alone was **1.1 million units**, **-11%** year over year and **-11%** versus January.
- **Regional comparison (Jan–Feb 2026 vs. the same period in 2025)**: China **1.1 million (-26%)**, Europe **0.6 million (+21%)**, North America **0.17 million (-36%)**, other markets **0.37 million (+84%)**.
- **Europe** became the main growth engine: Germany’s EV sales are **+26%** year to date; France **+30%**; Italy was **+23%** month over month in February and **+98%** year to date. Italian household subsidies reach up to **€11,000**, and small businesses up to **€20,000**.
- **North America** remains under clear pressure: although February was **+8%** month over month, year-to-date sales are still **-36%**. Among US automakers, Ford BEV sales were **-70%**, Honda **-81%**, Kia **-52%**; SK On cut **37%** of jobs at its Georgia plant.
- **China**: February EV sales were **-32%** year over year, but exports were strong: overseas exports in the first two months of 2026 **exceeded 500,000 units**, **more than doubling** year over year.
- **Other markets** grew the fastest: February was **+78%** year over year; South Korea’s EV sales **more than tripled** month over month to **more than 37,200 units**, with monthly sales exceeding **30,000 units** for the first time and EV market share reaching **30%** for the first time. Overall, the article’s strongest conclusion is: **regions where subsidies and policy support have been restored are seeing the strongest growth, while regions with tighter policy are slowing significantly**.
