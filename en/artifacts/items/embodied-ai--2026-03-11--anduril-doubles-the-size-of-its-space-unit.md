# Anduril doubles the size of its space unit

## Summary
This article reports that Anduril is acquiring space intelligence company ExoAnalytic Solutions in order to rapidly expand its national security space capabilities. The core value is bringing ExoAnalytic’s global deep-space sensor network, missile warning software, and simulation and tracking capabilities into Anduril’s space business.

## Problem
- The article focuses on this problem: national security space missions require stronger **space situational awareness**, satellite activity monitoring, missile warning, and missile defense capabilities.
- This matters because modern military operations depend heavily on space assets, and persistent, high-fidelity monitoring of deep-space targets directly affects warning, command and control, and fire allocation.
- For Anduril, the problem also includes how to enter and expand more quickly in the US Space Force-related market and capability landscape.

## Approach
- The core approach is very straightforward: **acquire ExoAnalytic rather than build from scratch**, and integrate its existing sensor network, software, and algorithms into Anduril.
- The key assets being brought in include a global telescope network, missile warning and missile defense software, and modeling, simulation, and target-tracking algorithms for classified national security space programs.
- ExoAnalytic has the “world’s largest commercial telescope network,” enabling persistent, high-fidelity awareness of deep space on a global scale.
- These assets will be used to strengthen Anduril’s product and delivery capabilities in areas such as **space domain awareness、battle management、fire control**.

## Results
- The clearest quantified outcome is that the acquisition will increase Anduril’s headcount focused on space projects to **more than double** its previous size.
- The scale of infrastructure brought by ExoAnalytic is a commercial telescope network with **more than 400 systems deployed worldwide**.
- The article says the network can provide **persistent, high-fidelity awareness** of deep space, but does not provide specific metrics for precision, coverage, or refresh rate.
- There are no paper-style experimental results, benchmark datasets, quantitative performance gains, or comparison figures against competing methods.
- The strongest concrete claim in the article is that the acquisition will **accelerate** Anduril’s support for national security space customers and strengthen its competitiveness in space situational awareness, battle management, and fire control.
