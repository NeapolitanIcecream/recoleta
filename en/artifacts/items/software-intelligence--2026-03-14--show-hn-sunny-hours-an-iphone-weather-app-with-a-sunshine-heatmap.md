# Show HN: Sunny Hours – an iPhone weather app with a sunshine heatmap

## Summary
This is not a research paper, but a description of a weather app for iPhone/iPad. Its main feature is an hour-by-hour "sunshine heatmap" that helps users quickly determine when the sun will appear over the next 10 days, and it offers several premium features such as AI daily briefings and multi-location comparison.

## Problem
- The problem it aims to solve is that conventional weather forecasts are too broad, making it hard for users to quickly know "exactly which hours will be sunny."
- This matters because plans such as going out, walking, photography, and exercise often depend on short windows of sunlight rather than generic descriptions like "cloudy/sunny."
- The copy also implies a usability problem: traditional weather interfaces are cluttered, and users need more direct sunshine visualization with lower cognitive load.

## Approach
- The core method is simple: compress how sunny each upcoming hour will be into a visual heatmap, where sunnier periods stand out more, allowing users to spot the best sunshine window at a glance.
- The app provides hour-by-hour sunshine forecasts and a 10-day overview, along with basic weather features such as current temperature, cloud cover, search for any city, and current-location support.
- Premium features build on this with AI Sun Briefing, Sun Score, best sunshine window recommendations, a rain overlay, morning notifications, and saving/comparing multiple locations.
- From a product-mechanism perspective, it does not propose a new weather prediction model, but rather reorganizes and presents existing weather information in a task-oriented way centered on sunshine.
