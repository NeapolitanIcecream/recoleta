# Global EV sales hit 1.1M – Europe surges while the US slides

## Summary
This article summarizes global electric vehicle sales data for February 2026, noting that overall global volume weakened while regional divergence intensified significantly. The core conclusion is: policy, subsidies, and trade rules are shaping EV market trajectories across regions more strongly than demand alone.

## Problem
- The question the article seeks to address is: **Is the global EV market actually slowing overall, or is it simply experiencing regional divergence**? This matters for supply chains, investment, and policy judgment.
- This is important because looking only at the decline in global total sales could lead to misjudging the EV transition trend and overlooking key sources of growth such as Europe, South Korea, and Chinese exports.
- The article also attempts to explain **why performance differs so much across regions**, mainly attributing it to changes in subsidy policies, tax adjustments, and trade arrangements.

## Approach
- The article is based on **EV sales data for February 2026 and year-to-date 2026** released by Benchmark Mineral Intelligence, comparing the global market, China, Europe, North America, and other regions.
- The core mechanism can be understood simply as: **where subsidies and policy support exist, EV sales are more likely to grow; where support is weakened or taxes are raised, sales are more likely to decline**.
- The article further explains regional shifts through country-level cases, such as subsidy-driven growth in Germany, France, and Italy; declining sales in North America, especially the United States; and cooling demand in China due to the reimposition of purchase taxes and adjustments to trade-in policies.
- At the same time, it uses exports, company sales, and supply-chain events to illustrate market transmission effects, such as surging Chinese exports, sharp declines in BEV sales by U.S. automakers, and layoffs at SK On.

## Results
- **Global**: Global EV sales in February 2026 were **1.1 million units**; **-11%** year-over-year and **-11%** versus January. The cumulative total for the first two months of 2026 was **2.2 million units**, **-8%** year-over-year.
- **Regional year-to-date (January–February 2026 vs. the same period in 2025)**: China **1.1 million (-26%)**, Europe **0.6 million (+21%)**, North America **0.17 million (-36%)**, other regions **0.37 million (+84%)**.
- **Europe**: **+1%** month-over-month in February; Germany **+26%** year-to-date, France **+30%**, and Italy **+23%** month-over-month in February and **+98%** year-to-date. Italy’s subsidies reach up to **€11,000** (households) and **€20,000** (small businesses).
- **North America / United States**: North America was **+8%** month-over-month in February, but still **-36%** year-to-date. BEV sales by U.S. automakers fell sharply: Ford **-70%**, Honda **-81%**, Kia **-52%**; SK On cut **37%** of staff at its Georgia plant.
- **Canada and China**: Canada’s EV market is **-23%** year-to-date; an import agreement allows China-made EVs to enter at a relatively low tariff of **6.1%**, and up to **24,500** import permits will be issued within six months. China’s EV sales in February were **-32%** year-over-year, but EV exports in the first two months exceeded **500,000 units**, **more than doubling** year-over-year.
- **Other regions / South Korea**: The rest of the world was **+78%** year-over-year in February; South Korea’s EV sales **more than tripled** month-over-month to **37,200+ units**, surpassing **30,000** units in a single month for the first time, and EV penetration reached **30%** for the first time.
