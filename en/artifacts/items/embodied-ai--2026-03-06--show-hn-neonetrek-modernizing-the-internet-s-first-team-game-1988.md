# Show HN: NeoNetrek – modernizing the internet's first team game (1988)

## Summary
NeoNetrek is a modern web remake of Netrek, an early 1988 internet multiplayer team game, focused on real-time browser-based interstellar team combat. It addresses the accessibility and modern deployment problems of an old game, rather than introducing new models or algorithms from academic research.

## Problem
- Traditional early networked games often depend on local installation, specific platforms, or outdated clients, creating a high barrier to entry for new players.
- If a real-time team combat game lacks modern Web delivery, it is difficult to be broadly experienced and shared in today’s browser environment.
- Making the “internet’s first team game” directly playable on modern devices helps preserve game history and expand access to multiplayer online cooperative play.

## Approach
- The core idea is simple: migrate classic Netrek gameplay to the modern Web to deliver a real-time multiplayer team battle experience where you can “just open a webpage and play.”
- The system runs in the browser and uses **WebSocket** for real-time online communication, with no downloads, plugins, or installation required.
- It preserves the original’s core multiplayer structure: 4 empire factions, up to **16-player team** play, a persistent galactic battlefield, and the capture of planets and defense of homeworlds.
- It retains and modernizes multiple ship and weapon/ability systems, including Scout, Starbase, torpedoes, phasers, tractors, cloaking, and plasma weapons.
