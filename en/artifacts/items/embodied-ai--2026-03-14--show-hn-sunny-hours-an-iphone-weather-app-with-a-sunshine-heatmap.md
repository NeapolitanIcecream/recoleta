# Show HN: Sunny Hours – an iPhone weather app with a sunshine heatmap

## Summary
This is not a research paper, but an introduction to a weather app. It focuses on showing sunlight conditions hour by hour over the next 10 days, using a heatmap to help users quickly find the time periods most likely to be sunny.

## Problem
- The problem it addresses is that conventional weather forecasts are too broad, making it hard for users to quickly determine **exactly which hours** will have sunshine.
- This matters because activities such as going out, exercising, photography, and drying clothes often depend on short windows of sunlight, and coarse-grained information like “cloudy/sunny” is not sufficient.
- The text also emphasizes reducing the friction of “guessing when the sun will come out,” allowing users to plan the current day and the next few days more intuitively.

## Approach
- The core mechanism is an **hourly sunlight heatmap**: it visualizes how sunny each upcoming hour will be, with the sunniest periods highlighted directly.
- Rather than emphasizing complex charts, it uses the simplest visual encoding so users can see at a glance “when the clouds will clear, when the sun will appear, and how long it will last.”
- Core features include: hour-by-hour sunshine forecasts, a 10-day overview, current temperature and cloud cover, and current-location/global city search.
- Premium features include: AI Sun Briefing (sunlight summary), Sun Score (daily ratings and 7-day average), best sunlight window recommendations (peak sun %, rain risk, wind conditions), morning notifications, rain overlay, and saved multiple locations.
