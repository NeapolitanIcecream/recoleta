# Show HN: MoatRadar – AI investment research through Warren Buffett's principles

## Summary
This is not an academic paper, but an introduction to an AI product for investment research. It claims to use investment frameworks from Buffett, Graham, Damodaran, and others to generate research conclusions on stocks, startups, and crypto assets in about 30 seconds.

## Problem
- The problem it aims to solve is that investors find it difficult to quickly and systematically screen a large number of global assets using classic value investing and fundamental analysis frameworks.
- This matters because research across markets and asset classes is usually time-consuming and information is fragmented, and ordinary users struggle to apply the methodologies of multiple investment masters at the same time.
- The text also implicitly aims to lower the barrier to research: users can try it without an account, and complex analysis is compressed into a single search workflow.

## Approach
- The core mechanism is simple: the user inputs a research target, the system calls **GPT-4o**, combines it with the external financial data source **Financial Modeling Prep**, and generates analysis according to 40+ investors and 80+ investment frameworks.
- It covers three asset classes: **stocks、startups、crypto**, and claims to provide opportunity discovery results in a single search.
- Platform-side capabilities include scanning **40,000+ stocks** across **70+ global exchanges**, and the interface shows a workflow of “scanning global markets using the investor's framework.”
- The output is positioned as “AI-generated ideas for research only,” meaning it is more of a research assistant and idea generator rather than a verifiable automated investment decision system.

## Results
- The text does not provide academically meaningful quantitative experimental results, benchmark datasets, comparison methods, or accuracy/return metrics, so there are **no verifiable quantitative research results**.
- The strongest specific product claim is that analysis results can be returned within **30 seconds**.
- Coverage claims include scanning **40,000+ stocks** across **70+ global exchanges**.
- Method scale claims include using the principles of **40+** investors and **80+ investor frameworks**.
- Commercial and usage-related figures include: **2 free searches**, **1 search = €1 = up to 20 opportunities**, and **3 free credits** for new users.
- Because it lacks comparisons with human analysis, traditional screeners, or other AI investment research tools, its relative performance gains cannot be determined.
