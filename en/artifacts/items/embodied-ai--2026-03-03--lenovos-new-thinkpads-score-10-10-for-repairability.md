# Lenovo’s new ThinkPads score 10/10 for repairability

## Summary
This article introduces Lenovo's new ThinkPad T14 Gen 7 and T16 Gen 5, which received iFixit's **10/10 repairability score**, positioning them as an important milestone for repairable design in mainstream business laptops. The core significance is that high repairability is no longer just a niche selling point, but is beginning to appear in mainstream models purchased at scale.

## Problem
- The problem the article addresses is: **how to enable mainstream business laptops to achieve genuinely high, practical repairability without sacrificing performance, reliability, thermals, and form factor**.
- This matters because the ThinkPad T series is used in large-scale enterprise procurement, and ease of repair directly affects **downtime, total cost of ownership, device lifespan, and e-waste**.
- Traditional approaches often consider repairability only late in development, by which point structure, materials, and layout have already been locked in, making meaningful improvements difficult.

## Approach
- The core mechanism is simple: **move “repairability” earlier in the product design process, rather than trying to retrofit it after the hardware is mostly finalized**.
- Lenovo collaborated with iFixit during development, using repeated teardowns, evaluations, and feedback to drive optimization of many **small but critical** structural decisions, rather than relying on a single major redesign.
- Specific design features include: **an almost tool-free battery replacement, a standard M.2 SSD, an extremely easy-to-replace keyboard, maintainable LPCAMM2 memory, simplified display repair, a modular thermal system with independently replaceable fans, and fully modular Thunderbolt ports**.
- At the organizational level, Lenovo involved design, engineering, service, quality, and sustainability teams from day one to balance repairability with the multiple constraints of business devices.

## Results
- The article claims that the new **ThinkPad T14 Gen 7** and **ThinkPad T16 Gen 5** received **10/10** repairability scores in iFixit's rating system; this is the **first time the T series has earned a perfect score**.
- Compared with earlier products: two years ago, the repairability-focused **ThinkPad T14** received **9/10**, so this represents an improvement from **9/10 to 10/10**.
- The article does not provide traditional academic-paper-style experimental metrics, datasets, or statistically significant results; **it does not publicly quantify comparisons such as percentage reductions in repair time, failure rate, or cost**.
- The strongest concrete outcome claim is that the new T series includes several highly repairable component designs, including **an almost tool-free battery, an extremely easy-to-replace keyboard, independently replaceable fans, and fully modular Thunderbolt ports**.
- The score is currently described as a **provisional 10/10**, pending final confirmation once Lenovo's official parts and repair documentation are available; the article says it “fully expects” that support to arrive soon.
- The article also acknowledges remaining limitations, such as **not all I/O being fully modular, the Wi‑Fi module not being especially repair-friendly, and deeper display panel/camera repairs still involving adhesive structures**, showing that 10/10 does not mean absolute perfection.
