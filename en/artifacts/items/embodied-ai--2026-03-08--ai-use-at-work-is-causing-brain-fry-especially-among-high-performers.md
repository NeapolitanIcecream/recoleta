# AI Use at Work Is Causing "Brain Fry", Especially Among High Performers

## Summary
This article outlines a survey of nearly 1,500 full-time U.S. workers, showing that frequent use of AI to boost work output can lead to a form of mental fatigue called “AI brain fry.” The research emphasizes that this burden not only affects employee well-being, but may also harm organizational decision quality and retention.

## Problem
- The problem the research aims to address is whether employees who use AI tools continuously and intensively at work experience mental fatigue that exceeds their cognitive load, and why that matters.
- This matters because AI is marketed as a tool for improving efficiency, but in practice it may instead intensify fatigue, reduce judgment quality, and increase turnover intentions through information overload, frequent task switching, and supervising multiple AI systems.
- The article specifically notes that high-performing employees are also affected, with the effects being more pronounced in roles such as marketing, software development, HR, finance, and IT.

## Approach
- The core method was a survey of nearly 1,500 full-time U.S. workers, collecting information on how frequently and in what ways they use AI at work, as well as their subjective experiences of resulting mental fatigue.
- The study defines this phenomenon as “mental fatigue that results from excessive use of, interaction with, and/or oversight of AI tools beyond one’s cognitive capacity,” and names it “AI brain fry.”
- Put simply, the study’s mechanistic explanation is that AI does not always directly do the work for people; often, employees must constantly switch between multiple tools, review outputs, correct errors, and supervise agents. This additional cognitive burden of “managing AI” can make the mind feel crowded, noisy, and exhausted.
- The study also analyzed the correlations between the intensity of AI oversight and mental fatigue, decision fatigue, and turnover intention.

## Results
- Among nearly 1,500 full-time U.S. workers, **14%** of respondents said they had experienced “AI brain fry”-style mental fatigue.
- High-intensity oversight of AI tools predicted **12% more** mental fatigue among employees, one of the main drivers emphasized in the article.
- Employees reporting “AI brain fry” showed an increase of **nearly 10%** in intention to leave their company.
- Employees experiencing brain fry also showed a **33%** increase in decision fatigue, which the article says could lead to decision losses worth millions of dollars annually for large enterprises.
- The results mainly come from correlational survey analysis rather than randomized controlled experiments; the article does not provide experimental comparison figures against clear external baseline models or intervention methods.
