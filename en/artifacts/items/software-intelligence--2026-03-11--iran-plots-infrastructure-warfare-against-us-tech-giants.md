# Iran plots 'infrastructure warfare' against US tech giants

## Summary
This is a news report about geopolitical conflict escalating into attacks on cloud and technology infrastructure, rather than an academic research paper. The core message is that Iran has publicly listed the Middle Eastern offices, R&D, and datacenter facilities of multiple US technology companies as potential retaliatory targets, highlighting the risk of the weaponization of digital infrastructure.

## Problem
- The issue discussed in the report is that state conflict is expanding from traditional military targets to **technology infrastructure** such as **cloud datacenters, R&D centers, and corporate technical facilities**.
- This matters because cloud platforms and regional datacenters support large volumes of enterprise and public services, and if attacked could trigger **regional service disruptions, supply chain failures, and disaster recovery migration pressure**.
- The significance for the software and intelligent systems field is that AI, cloud services, code, and production systems increasingly depend on centralized infrastructure, and its physical vulnerability can directly affect continuity of software development and operations.

## Approach
- This is not a methods paper; the mechanism described is **deterrence and escalation of infrastructure warfare**: Iran used state media/Telegram to publish a “new targets” list, defining regional facilities of tech companies as legitimate strike targets.
- The list covers offices, datacenters, and R&D facilities, and describes them by company, facility type, location, and function, showing that target selection is centered on **technology and cloud service support capabilities**.
- The report also mentions that Iran had previously attacked three AWS datacenters in the Middle East and claimed this was related to support for US military operations.
- The aftermath is described as affected vendors and customers initiating **cross-region failover and disaster recovery**, reflecting the emergency mechanisms of modern cloud architectures amid geopolitical conflict.

## Results
- The article says Iran listed **29** potential target locations across **Bahrain, Israel, Qatar, and the United Arab Emirates**.
- The numbers of facilities named are: **Amazon 5**, **Microsoft 5**, **IBM 6**, **Palantir 3**, **Google 4**, **Nvidia 3**, **Oracle 3**.
- The report says Iran had previously attacked **3 AWS datacenters**: **1 in Bahrain, 2 in the United Arab Emirates**; AWS also said drones struck **2** datacenters in the United Arab Emirates.
- In terms of impact, the report does not provide formal performance metrics or loss figures, but it explicitly claims the attacks caused **many cloud providers in the region to go offline**, and prompted **Snowflake, Red Hat, EMQX** to advise customers to execute disaster recovery and migrate to other regions.
- There are no academic experiments, benchmark datasets, or reproducible experimental results; the strongest concrete conclusion is: **technology infrastructure has now been publicly incorporated into a state-level retaliatory target list, and real-world attacks have already disrupted regional cloud availability.**
