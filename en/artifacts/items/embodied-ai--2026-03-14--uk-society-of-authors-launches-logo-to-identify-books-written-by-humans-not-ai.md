# UK Society of Authors launches logo to identify books written by humans not AI

## Summary
The UK Society of Authors has introduced the “Human Authored” label to help readers identify books written by human authors rather than generated by AI. This is a voluntary industry certification scheme focused on transparency in the publishing market and protection of authors’ rights, rather than a technical research paper.

## Problem
- AI-generated content is increasing in the book market, making it difficult for readers to distinguish between human writing and machine-generated works.
- At present, there are no mandatory government measures requiring technology companies to clearly label AI-generated content.
- Authors are concerned that their copyright-protected works are being used by AI companies to train models without permission or payment, harming creators’ rights and the creative ecosystem.

## Approach
- The UK Society of Authors launched a voluntary registration program allowing authors to register their works and download the “Human Authored” label.
- Authors can display the label on the back cover of their books to signal to readers that the book was created by a human rather than generated by AI.
- In essence, this mechanism is an industry self-certification and market-labeling scheme; simply put, it gives “books written by real people” a unified, recognizable label.
- The scheme also raises public awareness through endorsements from well-known authors and its launch at a book fair, while linking to broader advocacy around AI-training copyright issues.

## Results
- The article does not provide technical experiments, benchmark tests, or academic metrics, so there are no model performance figures to report.
- Its main reported outcome is that the UK Society of Authors launched the UK trade association’s “first of its kind” human-authored labeling scheme.
- Survey data shows that **82%** of the association’s author members expressed interest in participating in the “Human Authored” certification program.
- The article says the scheme is practically helpful both for readers identifying the source of works and for authors promoting the value of human creativity, but it does not provide adoption rates, conversion rates, or market-impact data.
- A related protest action mentioned that **thousands of authors** participated in publishing an “empty book,” *Don’t Steal This Book*, to oppose AI companies’ unauthorized use of works to train models.
