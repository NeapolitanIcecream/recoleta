# AI generates nude images that outrank real photographs in sexual appeal

## Summary
This study compared subjective responses to real nude photographs of women versus various artificially generated/stylized nude images, and found that **AI-generated nude images surpassed real photographs in aesthetics, sexual attractiveness, and emotional pleasantness**, although real photographs were still considered the most realistic. The significance of the study is that it shows generative AI has begun to change people's perceptual benchmarks for beauty, realism, and sexual appeal.

## Problem
- The study aimed to address how people perceive **real photographs** versus different types of nude images such as **AI, CG, surgically enhanced women, sex dolls, and hentai** in today's digital pornographic/erotic visual environment.
- This matters because the visual sexual materials encountered by younger generations have been extensively reshaped by digital retouching, fictional characters, and AI, and the stimulus materials used in past psychological research may no longer represent current preferences.
- The study also asks whether, as images become increasingly realistic, controllable, and idealized, **“real” still remains equivalent to “more attractive.”**

## Approach
- The researchers conducted a nationwide online survey in the Czech Republic, recruiting **649** adults who identified as being attracted to women; the sample was predominantly male, with an additional **45 women**.
- Participants viewed six categories of static nude female images: **real photographs, AI-generated images, CG images, surgically enhanced women, silicone sex dolls, and hentai**; each category included **5** appearance types to control for differences in hair-color and body-type preferences.
- The research team attempted to match the images across categories in pose, body shape, and appearance, and used post-processing to standardize skin tone and remove distracting factors such as tattoos/accessories in order to improve comparability.
- Participants used **0–100** sliders to rate the images on **realism, sexual attractiveness, and aesthetic attractiveness**, and used a standard **5-point** pictorial scale to assess emotional pleasantness (valence); image order was randomized.

## Results
- For **realism**, **real photographs ranked first**, and **AI images ranked second**; they were followed by **CG, surgically enhanced women, sex dolls, and hentai**.
- Although less “real” than actual photographs, **AI images ranked first in aesthetic attractiveness**, and also ranked first in **sexual attractiveness** and **emotional pleasantness**; **real photographs ranked second on all three measures**.
- **Surgically enhanced women** scored lowest in **aesthetic attractiveness** and **emotional pleasantness**; **hentai** and **sex dolls** also ranked relatively low overall, and most participants found these low-realism categories “somewhat unpleasant.”
- Regarding gender differences, **men generally gave higher ratings across all categories**; women gave lower ratings overall, but the category ranking pattern was broadly consistent with that of men.
- Regarding age differences, **older participants** gave higher ratings for aesthetic attractiveness/sexual attractiveness/pleasantness to **real photographs, AI images, and CG images**; **younger participants** showed a clear preference for **hentai**.
- The text does not provide **specific means, standard deviations, effect sizes, or significance values** for each category, so the strongest quantitative conclusions that can be confirmed are mainly the sample size **649**, female subsample **45**, image categories **6**, appearance types per category **5**, and the relative rankings on each measure.
