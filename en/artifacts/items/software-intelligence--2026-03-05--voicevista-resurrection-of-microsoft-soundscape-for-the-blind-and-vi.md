# VoiceVista – Resurrection of Microsoft Soundscape for the Blind and VI

## Summary
VoiceVista is a continuation and reconstruction of Microsoft's discontinued Soundscape, providing audio- and location-based environmental awareness and navigation assistance for blind and low-vision users. Its significance lies in restoring and continuing an accessibility capability that helps users build mental maps of space and improve confidence in independent travel.

## Problem
- Blind and low-vision users often find it difficult to obtain continuous, low-burden spatial environmental information in unfamiliar settings, which affects independent navigation and sense of safety.
- Microsoft Soundscape has been discontinued, causing the relevant user community to lose a critical accessible navigation tool.
- A sustainable, usable alternative that emphasizes privacy and openness is needed to restore this capability.

## Approach
- Revive and reimplement the discontinued open-source Microsoft Soundscape project, using an open-source foundation under the MIT License.
- Use advanced iOS audio technology to convey environmental and directional information to users through sound.
- Combine this with precise location services to provide cues about the surrounding environment as users move, helping them form a "mental map."
- The design goal is not simply to provide routes, but to enhance overall environmental awareness and provide reassurance and navigation support in unfamiliar situations.
