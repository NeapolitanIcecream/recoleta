# ISTQB Certifications Under the Lens: Their Contributions to the Software-Testing Profession; and AI-assisted Synthesis of Practitioners' Endorsements and Criticisms

## Summary
This paper examines the professional value and main controversies of ISTQB, the most widely adopted software testing certification worldwide, and uses an AI-assisted multivocal literature review combined with expert re-evaluation to synthesize practitioner perspectives. The conclusion is that ISTQB has clear value in professional recognition, shared terminology, and knowledge structuring, but its representativeness of practical skills and fit with industry needs remain under ongoing debate.

## Problem
- The paper aims to address the question: **What value do ISTQB certifications actually bring to the software testing profession, and what shortcomings widely criticized by practitioners do they have**.
- This matters because ISTQB has issued **1.2M+ certifications** and organized **1.4M+ exams** across **130+ countries**, giving it global influence on hiring, training, and career development.
- If companies treat certification as a proxy for skill, while the certification itself does not fully align with real testing ability, this can affect hiring, training investment, and software quality governance.

## Approach
- The authors conducted an **AI-assisted multivocal literature review (MLR)**, combining practitioner opinions from academic and grey literature, and synthesizing **supportive views (RQ1)** and **critical views (RQ2)** separately.
- The study used **ChatGPT deep research** for evidence collection and synthesis, but emphasized **continuous human oversight, quality assurance, transparent documentation, and an explainable process**, with execution details disclosed in the appendix.
- To go beyond merely “collecting opinions,” the authors also invited **4 independent experts** to evaluate whether these supportive views were stated precisely and whether these criticisms were fair.
- The paper also supplements this with a structured overview of the ISTQB system itself, including its **23 certifications / 22+ syllabi**, role-oriented design, foundation in Bloom’s taxonomy, and its contribution to the software testing body of knowledge (STBoK).

## Results
- The main practitioner endorsements focus on: **career development, job-market advantage, professional recognition, shared terminology, and structured knowledge**; the main criticisms focus on: **being overly theoretical, lacking practical relevance, offering limited support for agile and automation contexts, and failing to effectively measure real testing skills**.
- The main conclusion from expert re-evaluation is that **many supportive views are precise**; and several criticisms are not simply “inaccurate,” but rather reflect tensions between different schools of practice within the software testing field. The abstract does not provide more fine-grained numerical ratings.
- The strongest quantitative background data provided by the paper include: since 2002, ISTQB has covered **130+ countries**, conducted **1.4M+ exams**, and issued **1.2M+ certifications**; its certification portfolio includes **23 certifications** and **22+ syllabi**.
- The paper excerpt **does not provide** the quantitative performance metrics commonly seen in traditional experimental papers (such as accuracy, F1, significance tests, or numerical comparisons with baseline methods). Its main results are **qualitative evidence-based conclusions** derived through systematic synthesis, along with re-evaluation judgments by **4 experts**.
