# Dario Amodei calls OpenAI’s messaging around military deal ‘straight up lies’

## Summary
This article is not a research paper, but an industry news report focusing on the public disagreement between Anthropic and OpenAI over the terms of cooperation with the U.S. Department of Defense. The core dispute concerns the military’s access rights to AI for “any lawful use” and the safety boundaries around that access.

## Problem
- The issue discussed in the article is whether AI companies, when cooperating with defense/military institutions, should allow their models to be used for **any lawful use**, and whether that framing is sufficient to prevent abuse.
- This matters because it involves high-risk scenarios such as domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons, reflecting whether governance and safety commitments in frontier AI deployment are genuinely credible.
- The article also points out that the boundary of so-called “lawful use” can change as laws change, so contractual wording may be insufficient as a long-term constraint.

## Approach
- This is not a technical methods paper; its core mechanism is to constrain the military use of AI through **contractual restrictions and policy red lines**.
- Anthropic’s position is that it required the DoD to explicitly commit not to use its AI for **domestic mass surveillance** or **autonomous weapons**, otherwise it would not accept unrestricted access.
- OpenAI’s position is that its contract allows use for **all lawful purposes**, and it claims to have explicitly excluded domestic mass surveillance in the contract because that use does not currently fall within lawful use.
- The substance of the dispute is the difference between two governance mechanisms: **restrictions based on explicit red lines** vs **permission based on current legal definitions**.

## Results
- No research experiments, benchmark datasets, or model performance metrics are provided, so there are **no quantitative technical results** to summarize.
- The most specific figure in the article is that Anthropic previously already had a **$200 million** military contract.
- The article says that after OpenAI reached an agreement with the DoD, **ChatGPT uninstalls increased by 295%**, presented as a signal of negative public reaction, but it does not provide fuller methodological background or baseline details.
- The article also mentions that Anthropic rose to **#2** in app store rankings, as indirect evidence that its public image benefited.
- The strongest substantive claim is not a technical breakthrough but a governance comparison: Anthropic claims it rejected unrestricted access for “any lawful use” to avoid abuse, while Amodei accuses OpenAI’s safety messaging of being “straight up lies.”
