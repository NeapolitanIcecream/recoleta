# The role of team diversity in AI systems development

## Summary
This is a qualitative study based on real-world enterprise AI projects, exploring how team diversity helps identify and mitigate AI bias. The paper’s core contribution is the synthesis of six roles of team diversity from interviews with 25 practitioners, along with recommendations for integrating fairness into development practices.

## Problem
- AI systems often amplify social inequality through bias in data, design, and development decisions, but existing research has mostly focused on technical fixes such as data, models, and algorithms.
- How the social factor of team composition affects the identification and mitigation of AI bias, especially in real software engineering environments, still lacks empirical study.
- This matters because if differences in perspectives within development teams are ignored, AI may continue to produce discrimination and unfairness in high-impact scenarios such as hiring, credit, content moderation, and facial recognition.

## Approach
- The paper uses the qualitative **Grounded Theory** method, without assuming a fixed theoretical framework in advance, and instead derives the mechanisms through which team diversity matters from practitioners’ experiences.
- The data comes from 4 AI projects within a large software company operating in Brazil and Portugal, covering scenarios such as sign language translation, digital twins/predictive models, educational LLMs, and computer-vision-based facial recognition.
- The study collected data through 3 rounds of iterative semi-structured interviews: a total of **25** software professionals participated, with an additional **3** open-ended questionnaires as supplements; interviews lasted **23–42 minutes**, totaling **4 hours 49 minutes** of audio and producing **504 pages** of transcripts.
- The researchers used line-by-line coding, focused coding, and theoretical coding, and reached theoretical saturation in the 3rd round. They ultimately identified **6 key roles** of team diversity: diversifying perspectives for bias identification, bringing empathy to AI development, addressing systemic discrimination, supporting inclusive and participatory decision-making, serving as a safeguard against bias, and broadening problem-solving approaches.

## Results
- The most central empirical result is the identification of **6 roles** of team diversity, used to explain how it influences bias identification and fairness practices in AI development.
- The study sample includes **4 AI projects**, **25 interviewees**, and **3 questionnaires**; the data reached theoretical saturation after **3 rounds** of collection, indicating that the authors believe the main themes were sufficiently covered.
- The paper does not report **quantitative performance improvements** such as accuracy, F1, or AUC on traditional machine learning benchmarks, nor does it provide a numerical comparison experiment of “diverse teams vs. non-diverse teams.”
- The strongest concrete claim is that diverse teams can detect biases hidden in data, requirements, and design assumptions earlier, and reduce unfairness risks through empathy, participatory decision-making, and multi-perspective review.
- The paper also argues that diversity not only helps identify algorithmic bias, but also helps align AI development with broader social goals such as **fairness, equity, inclusivity**, thereby reducing potential harm and “software fairness debt.”
