# Pick one of catastrophic or equitable. Are founder clean breaks possible?

## Summary

The core discussion is: when the company is not yet stable and the person is the only technical founder, is it possible to leave early and retain or dispose of vested equity without harming the company and in a relatively fair way.

## Problem
- The issue under discussion is whether there is a **non-catastrophic and relatively fair** path for the only technical cofounder to leave and switch careers.
- This matters because such a departure would affect company continuity, investor confidence, the team’s execution capacity, the handling of vested equity, and the long-term relationship between the founder and the company.
- Specific concerns include: there is no obvious successor, the company’s growth is mediocre, it has just completed a **$5M seed** financing, and the product is still validating a new market; leaving at this point could be seen as a major shock to the company.
