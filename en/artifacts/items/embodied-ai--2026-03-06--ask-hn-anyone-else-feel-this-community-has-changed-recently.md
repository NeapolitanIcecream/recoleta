# Ask HN: Anyone else feel this community has changed recently?

## Summary
This is not a research paper, but a Hacker News community discussion post in which the author reflects on changes in HN's content and atmosphere in recent years. The core view is that the flood of AI topics, the decline in startup projects, and rising politicization and intolerance have reduced the community's appeal.

## Problem
- The issue under discussion is whether the Hacker News community has undergone negative changes in recent years, and why those changes have made long-time users lose interest.
- The author considers this important because HN used to be an important community for technical discussion, independent project launches, and open exchange; if quality declines, its value as a public technical space is diminished.
- Specific pain points include AI content overload, fewer showcases of solo or small-team startups, and a comment section that has become more one-sided and less tolerant of dissenting views.

## Approach
- This text does not propose a research method, but is based on the author's personal observations and subjective comparisons from long-term use of HN since 2010.
- The argument is very simple: it contrasts “past HN” with “current HN” and lists four signals of change—too much AI, fewer startups, more politicization, and an overall sense of nostalgia and loss.
- The author illustrates that they prefer traditional technical content over posts making subjective comparisons of AI models.
- In essence, the text is open-ended community feedback inviting other users to verify whether “it's just me” or not.

## Results
- There are no experiments, datasets, baselines, or quantitative metrics, so there are no verifiable quantitative results.
- The strongest specific claim is that the author has used HN continuously “since 2010” and has felt a “clear decline” in content quality over the “past few years.”
- The author claims that the share of AI-related content on HN has risen significantly, but provides no evidence such as post proportions, engagement data, or time-series trends.
- The author also claims that independent startup/project launch content has declined and that politicization and intolerance have increased, but likewise provides no statistical comparison or external evidence.
- Therefore, the output of this text is closer to a signal of community sentiment than to a research breakthrough or technical result.
