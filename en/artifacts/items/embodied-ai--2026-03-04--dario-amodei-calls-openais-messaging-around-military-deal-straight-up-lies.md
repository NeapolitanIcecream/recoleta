# Dario Amodei calls OpenAI’s messaging around military deal ‘straight up lies’

## Summary
This content is not a research paper, but a news report about a public dispute between Anthropic and OpenAI over the terms of cooperation with the U.S. Department of Defense. The core issue is that the two sides have conflicting descriptions of “lawful use” and safety red lines, which has triggered public reaction.

## Problem
- The issue discussed in the article is whether AI companies, when cooperating with the military, should allow models to be used for “any lawful use,” and whether that is sufficient to prevent abuse.
- This matters because the dispute involves high-risk uses such as domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons, and concerns the governance and deployment boundaries of advanced AI.
- The article also points out that relying solely on “what is currently permitted by law” may be insufficient, because laws and policies themselves can change.

## Approach
- This is not a technical methods paper and does not propose algorithms, models, or experimental mechanisms.
- In the form of a news report, the article recounts internal memos, public company statements, and differences in contract wording, comparing Anthropic and OpenAI’s differing positions on the terms of defense cooperation.
- Anthropic’s core position is that it required the DoD to explicitly commit not to use its AI for domestic mass surveillance or autonomous weapons; OpenAI, by contrast, said its contract allows “all lawful purposes” and stated that the relevant red lines were explicitly excluded.
