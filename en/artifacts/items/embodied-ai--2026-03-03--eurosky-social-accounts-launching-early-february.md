# Eurosky.social accounts – launching early February

## Summary
This is not a research paper, but a product/infrastructure launch announcement for **eurosky.social** accounts. Its core claim is to provide a portable, Europe-hosted social identity based on the AT Protocol, reducing platform lock-in and improving user control over data and identity.

## Problem
- Traditional social platforms usually bind **identity, data storage, and applications** within a single company, making users vulnerable to platform lock-in.
- Users’ accounts and social relationships may be affected by **arbitrary decisions by companies or governments**, lacking long-term independence and portability.
- For European users/institutions, **hosting location and legal governance** also matter, relating to data sovereignty and institutional trust.

## Approach
- Provide a **eurosky.social account** as a **unified social identity** usable across multiple applications.
- The account is based on the **AT Protocol**, which separates **identity, data storage, and frontend applications**. Put simply, the account no longer belongs to just one app.
- The account serves as the user’s **personal data server (PDS)** for storing posts, profile data, and connections; users can **migrate to other providers** in the future.
- Use an open protocol to achieve **interoperability and portability**, usable with applications such as Bluesky, Tangled, Sill, and Streamplace.
- Emphasize **management by a European non-profit, hosting on European cloud infrastructure, and governance under European law**, and states that data in accounts is currently public by default, with private data capabilities expected to be introduced to the AT Protocol in **2026**.

## Results
- The text **does not provide experiments, benchmarks, or academic evaluation results**, so there are no quantitative performance metrics to report.
- The strongest concrete claim is that **one identity can be used across dozens of apps**, and it explicitly names support for AT Protocol applications such as **Bluesky, Tangled, Sill, and Streamplace**.
- It claims users can **switch apps while keeping their network and data** to reduce platform lock-in, but provides no figures for migration success rate, latency, or cost.
- It states that current data under the protocol should be treated as **public data**, and that private data is expected in **2026**, but provides no formal roadmap metrics or validation results.
