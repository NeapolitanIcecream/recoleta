# The Trilingual Triad Framework: Integrating Design, AI, and Domain Knowledge in No-code AI Smart City Course

## Summary
This paper proposes the "Trilingual Triad" framework to explain how students can transform generative AI from a "tool" into a "collaborative teammate" in a no-code smart city course. The core argument is that only by coordinating design, AI, and domain knowledge can deeper AI literacy, reflective ability, and learner agency be fostered.

## Problem
- The paper addresses the problem that, in higher education, students often treat generative AI as a passive output tool rather than a knowledge partner they actively design, which limits the development of AI literacy, critical understanding, and learner agency.
- This matters because as generative AI enters education and professional workflows, simply knowing how to “use” AI is no longer enough; students need to learn how to customize, constrain, and evaluate AI systems for specific disciplinary tasks.
- The paper also focuses on an instructional design question: how to provide a replicable and theoretically grounded course model that enables students to build custom GPT systems for real tasks under no-code conditions.

## Approach
- The core method is simple: in a graduate course at SUTD, students were asked to design custom GPTs by hand, without writing code, around real smart city learning tasks, turning AI from a "ready-made tool" into a "collaborative teammate."
- The authors propose the "Trilingual Triad" mechanism: **domain knowledge determines how the AI should work, design determines how humans interact with AI, and AI extends human cognitive capacity**; the three must be orchestrated together rather than optimized separately.
- The study uses a **qualitative multi-case study** design, analyzing three student projects: Interview Companion GPT, Urban Observer GPT, and Buddy Buddy, with both in-depth single-case analysis and cross-case synthesis.
- The analytical framework revolves around three strands: **Design, AI, Domain Knowledge**, comparing how each case changed from "Before (general-purpose tool)" to "After (specialized teammate)."
- The theoretical grounding draws on SDT, Constructionism, TPACK, and Distributed Cognition to explain why "building AI by hand" can enhance student motivation, depth of understanding, and collaborative cognition.

## Results
- The paper **does not provide standardized benchmark tests, statistical significance testing, or unified quantitative metrics**; it does not report numerical performance results such as accuracy, F1, AUC, or sample-size effect sizes.
- The study analyzes **3 case projects**: Interview Companion GPT, Urban Observer GPT, and Buddy Buddy; this is the clearest empirical scale information provided in the paper.
- The specific claim for Interview Companion GPT is that it shifts from traditional peer/teacher role-play to multimodal interview simulation and immediate feedback supporting **text transcripts and real-time audio**, and has already been used in a real oral history interview context in collaboration with the **Oral History Centre of the National Archives of Singapore**.
- The specific claim for Urban Observer GPT is that it transforms urban observation from "unstructured notes" into a structured observation process supported by **computer vision + Observation Matrix**, and uses "Friendly Tutor"-style Socratic questioning to help students map urban theory onto field observations.
- The specific claim for Buddy Buddy is that it turns pre-class preparation in a flipped classroom from static PDF reading into **interactive personalized dialogue**, and generates an **Educator Report** for instructors to help them understand students' backgrounds and readiness.
- The strongest cross-case conclusion is that when **design, AI, and domain knowledge** are orchestrated together, students shift from passively using AI to actively creating AI, thereby improving **AI literacy, metacognition, learner agency**; however, these conclusions rely mainly on qualitative evidence rather than quantitative comparison.
