# Japanese government makes indie game devs eligible for grants up to $60k USD

## Summary
Japan’s Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry has launched the IP360 program, expanding eligibility for subsidies to include indie game developers and unincorporated teams, with support of up to 10 million yen available. Its goal is to support the development of new IP and overseas distribution, enhancing the international competitiveness of Japan’s content industry.

## Problem
- Traditional government funding often requires applicants to be registered companies, making it difficult for indie developers and small teams to obtain production and promotion funding.
- The Japanese government wants to expand overseas revenue from content industries such as anime and games, and therefore needs more new IP aimed at international markets.
- For indie developers, the costs of prototype development, localization, promotion, and attending overseas events are high, limiting the global reach of their works.

## Approach
- METI launched **IP360 (sanrokumaru)**, with its “New IP Creation Support (Startup Support)” open to individuals and unincorporated entities.
- The subsidy uses a **cost coverage model of up to 50%**, with a **cap of 10 million yen (about $62,000 USD)**.
- Funding covers a broad scope, including pre-production planning, full production, post-production, **localization**, and **promotion**.
- Typical reimbursable expenses include outsourced art/music, travel to overseas trade shows, and project communication costs.
- Applicants must submit a **prototype** and a **business plan including overseas distribution plans**; sequels, remakes, and other existing IP projects are not eligible, and the government states that it **does not intervene in creative content**.

## Results
- The text **does not provide quantified outcomes after implementation**, so there is currently no data on the actual number of grants awarded, sales growth, or overseas expansion conversions resulting from the policy.
- The clearly stated policy parameters are: **up to 10 million yen** in support per project, with a **maximum subsidy rate of 50%**.
- The program serves a broader Japanese government goal: increasing the content industry’s **annual overseas revenue to 20 trillion yen (about $125.5 billion USD)**.
- By **2025**, this goal had been elevated to **official national policy**.
- The strongest specific claim in the article is that, compared with common subsidy programs limited to company applicants, this program **notably opens access to individuals/indie developers for the first time**, while covering multi-stage costs from development through overseas promotion.
