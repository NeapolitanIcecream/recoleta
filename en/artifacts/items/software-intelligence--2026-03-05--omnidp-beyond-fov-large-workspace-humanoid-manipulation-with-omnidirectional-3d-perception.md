# OmniDP: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D Perception

## Summary
OmniDP proposes an end-to-end humanoid robot manipulation policy based on panoramic LiDAR point clouds, using 360° 3D perception to extend the manipulation workspace beyond the camera's field of view. Its core value is reducing failures, collisions, and deployment complexity caused by narrow FOV, occlusion, and frequent base repositioning.

## Problem
- Traditional RGB-D/depth cameras have narrow fields of view and are prone to self-occlusion, which limits humanoid robots to stable manipulation only within a small area directly in front of them; targets and obstacles outside the view are often missed.
- In scenarios with limited space where the robot cannot easily turn around or move its base, this perception bottleneck directly leads to task failure, increased collision risk, and greater control uncertainty.
- Existing methods for expanding perception, such as active vision or third-person-view cameras, often introduce additional mechanical complexity, calibration dependence, and real-time performance issues.

## Approach
- The core idea is to directly feed 360° point clouds generated by a head-mounted panoramic LiDAR into an end-to-end imitation learning policy, rather than relying on narrow-view RGB-D images.
- The perception encoder uses a 3D point cloud pyramid convolution and incorporates **Time-Aware Attention Pooling (TAP)**: point clouds within a short time window are aggregated together, each point is assigned a relative timestamp, and attention gives greater weight to more recent observations, thereby smoothing the temporal noise from sparse and flickering LiDAR signals.
- The encoded global feature is fed into a Diffusion Policy decoder to directly generate 28-dimensional upper-body joint actions; a 43-dimensional full-body proprioceptive state is also provided to support coordinated control.
- To train the policy, the paper builds a whole-body teleoperation system based on an XR headset and controllers, enabling efficient collection of large-workspace demonstration data with full-body coordination on the Unitree G1.
- During deployment, the point cloud is always represented in the LiDAR's own coordinate frame, so no additional extrinsic calibration is required, improving cross-environment adaptability.

## Results
- **Overall task performance (6 tasks, 120 trials total)**: OmniDP achieves **82/120**, significantly higher than DP **18/120**, DP3 **22/120**, and iDP3 **25/120**.
- **The advantage is most pronounced on out-of-view (OV) tasks**: in simulated Pour (OV), OmniDP scores **12/20**, while DP/DP3/iDP3 are all **0/20**; in real Hand Over (OV), OmniDP scores **12/20**, while all three baselines are **0/20**; in real Pour (OV), OmniDP scores **11/20**, while all baselines are **0/20**; in real Wipe (OV), OmniDP scores **16/20**, while all baselines are **0/20**.
- **It is also stronger on non-OV tasks**: on simulated Pick & Place, OmniDP scores **16/20**, outperforming DP **10/20**, DP3 **13/20**, and iDP3 **14/20**; on real Pick & Place, OmniDP scores **15/20**, outperforming DP **8/20**, DP3 **9/20**, and iDP3 **11/20**.
- **Collision avoidance experiment**: in scenarios where obstacles are outside the camera's field of view, OmniDP achieves a success rate of **14/20** and a collision rate of **5/20**; DP has **0/20** success and **20/20** collisions, DP3 has **0/20** success and **18/20** collisions, and iDP3 has **0/20** success and **18/20** collisions.
- **Generalization ability**: on Pick & Place variants, OmniDP achieves **13/20, 15/20, 12/20** under different instances, lighting conditions, and scenes, respectively; the corresponding results are **12/20, 12/20, 10/20** for iDP3, **7/20, 8/20, 7/20** for DP3, and **3/20, 4/20, 2/20** for DP.
- **Ablation study**: on the Hand Over task, full OmniDP achieves **12/20**; removing panoramic observation drops performance to **0/20**; removing TAP reduces it to **9/20**, showing that both 360° perception and time-aware pooling are critical.
