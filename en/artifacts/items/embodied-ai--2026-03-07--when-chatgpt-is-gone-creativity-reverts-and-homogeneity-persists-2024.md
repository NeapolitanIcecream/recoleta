# When ChatGPT is gone: Creativity reverts and homogeneity persists (2024)

## Summary
This paper examines whether ChatGPT’s boost to human creativity can persist, and concludes that the improvement is mainly temporary, while content homogenization continues to persist. Through a pre-registered 7-day experiment and a follow-up 30 days later, the authors found that once ChatGPT is removed, creative performance returns to baseline, but the convergence effect in generated content does not disappear immediately.

## Problem
- The paper addresses the question: **Can ChatGPT’s improvement in creative performance be retained after sustained use, and does it still remain after ChatGPT is no longer used?** This matters because many people assume generative AI will enhance human creative ability over the long term.
- Another key question is: **Does ChatGPT make people’s creative outputs increasingly similar and less diverse?** If so, this could weaken long-term innovation capacity, rather than merely affecting performance on a single task.
- This issue matters for education, creative work, and human-AI collaboration design, because short-term efficiency gains may come with long-term dependence on AI and a contraction of creativity.

## Approach
- The authors conducted a **pre-registered seven-day experiment** and a **follow-up survey/measurement 30 days after the experiment ended** to observe short-term and somewhat longer-term changes.
- The experiment included **61 college students**, divided into a **treatment group** (using ChatGPT in creative tasks) and a **control group** (completing the tasks independently).
- The data included **3302 creative ideas** and **427 creative solutions**, which were used to compare changes in creative performance with and without ChatGPT.
- The core mechanism is simple: **first test whether ChatGPT can “raise” people’s creative performance while it is being used, then test whether that increase remains when ChatGPT is unavailable; at the same time, check whether outputs become increasingly similar.**
- The study focuses in particular on two things: **whether creativity falls back to baseline**, and **whether homogenization continues even after ChatGPT disappears.**

## Results
- Based on **61 students**, **3302 creative ideas**, and **427 creative solutions**, the authors report that **ChatGPT’s creativity-enhancing effect remained consistently visible over five consecutive days of creative tasks.**
- But when ChatGPT was unavailable on **day 7**, and in the follow-up measurement on **day 30**, **human creative performance reverted to baseline**; the abstract does not provide more detailed quantitative metrics or specific score differences.
- The authors claim that using ChatGPT causes content to become **increasingly homogenized**, and that this **homogenization effect continues to persist even when ChatGPT is absent**.
- Therefore, the paper’s core conclusion is: **generative AI brings a temporary improvement in creative performance, not a stable enhancement of human creative capability.**
- The abstract **does not provide specific numerical results** (such as effect sizes, significance levels, exact score means, or precise differences versus baseline/control); the strongest quantitative information is mainly the sample size, data size, and time-point setup.
