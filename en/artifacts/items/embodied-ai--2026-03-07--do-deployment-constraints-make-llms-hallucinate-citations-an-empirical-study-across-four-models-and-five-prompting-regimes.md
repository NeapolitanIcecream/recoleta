# Do Deployment Constraints Make LLMs Hallucinate Citations? An Empirical Study across Four Models and Five Prompting Regimes

## Summary
This paper empirically studies the extent to which LLMs generate academic citations that “look correct but cannot be verified” under different deployment constraints. The conclusion is: the stronger the constraints, especially temporal-window constraints and combined constraints, the more citation verifiability declines significantly, and format compliance does not mean the citations are real.

## Problem
- The paper aims to solve the problem of how **prompting constraints in real-world deployment** (such as restricting publication years, requiring survey-style breadth, and forbidding claims of memorized training data) affect the **verifiability** of citations generated by LLMs.
- This matters because LLMs are already used in academic writing, systematic reviews, and software engineering evidence synthesis; if models fabricate references, those errors can propagate into research, tooling pipelines, and decision processes.
- Prior work mostly examined only a single prompting setup or a binary “real/fabricated” classification, which is insufficient to capture the large number of high-risk citations in real use that are “hard to automatically verify.”

## Approach
- The authors build a **closed-book** evaluation: with no retrieval, models are asked to generate a short academic passage and structured references based on 144 academic claims.
- They evaluate 4 models (Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o, LLaMA 3.1–8B, Qwen 2.5–14B) under 5 prompting conditions: Baseline, Temporal, Survey, Non-Disclosure, and Combo, for a total of **2,880 runs and 17,443 citations**.
- They design a **deterministic verification pipeline**: after parsing each citation, they query **Crossref + Semantic Scholar** using DOI/title, then perform weighted similarity matching on fields such as title, authors, year, and venue.
- Each citation is labeled into three categories: **Existing / Unresolved / Fabricated**, rather than forcing a binary classification; **Unresolved** means the automated system cannot judge reliably, but manual auditing shows that a substantial portion of these are actually fabricated.
- The pipeline is also manually validated: from a sample of 100 citations, agreement between automatic and manual labels is **75%**, with Cohen’s **κ=0.63**, indicating that the automatic evaluation is usable but conservative on Unresolved cases.

## Results
- **No model under any condition achieves a citation-level existence rate above 0.50**; the highest value in the paper is **0.475 for Claude Sonnet under the Survey condition**, showing that even the best model cannot guarantee that most citations are real.
- Under Baseline, proprietary models clearly outperform open-weight models: Claude **0.381**, GPT-4o **0.235**, while LLaMA **0.068** and Qwen **0.090**; the proprietary vs. open-weight gap is **Δ=+0.229, 95% CI [0.191, 0.266]**.
- **Temporal is the most damaging single constraint**: Claude’s existence rate drops from **0.381 to 0.119** (**Δ=-0.261**), and GPT-4o drops from **0.235 to 0.019** (**Δ=-0.216**); meanwhile, the temporal-violation rate remains extremely low, only **0.001–0.026**, indicating that models “follow the year-format constraint” but fail to produce real, verifiable literature.
- **The Combo condition is the worst**: Claude **0.106**, GPT-4o **0.005**, LLaMA **0.008**, Qwen **0.001**; at the same time, models still generate an average of **7.38–7.99** citations, exhibiting the pattern of “continuing to output many citations, but almost none are verifiable.”
- **The Survey condition widens the proprietary/open-weight gap**: this gap reaches the largest value in the study at **Δ=+0.310 [0.274, 0.349]**. Claude even improves from **0.381 to 0.475** (**Δ=+0.094**), whereas Qwen drops to **0.020**, and its fabricated rate reaches the highest in the paper at **0.547**.
- **Unresolved is the largest risk bucket**: across settings it accounts for **36%–61%**. In manual auditing, among 35 Unresolved citations, **16 were actually fabricated** and only **4 were existing**, suggesting that the fabricated rate reported in the paper is likely still a conservative estimate.
