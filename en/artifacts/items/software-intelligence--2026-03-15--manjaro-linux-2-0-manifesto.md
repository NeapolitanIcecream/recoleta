# Manjaro Linux 2.0 Manifesto

## Summary
This is not a technical research paper, but a manifesto for the governance restructuring of Manjaro Linux 2.0, aimed at rescuing a long-declining open-source project through reforms to its organization, assets, and decision-making mechanisms. Its core value lies in shifting the project from individual/company-centered control to community-led nonprofit governance.

## Problem
- The document seeks to address Manjaro’s long-term governance failures: loss of trust, loss of contributors, and the repeated recurrence of known operational issues without anyone truly fixing them.
- The authors argue that the root cause is a misalignment between leadership goals and those of developers/the community, with the project operating too heavily around a single individual and a commercial company, leading to a high concentration of permissions, resources, and infrastructure.
- This matters because governance failure directly affects the distribution’s reliability, use of funds, contributor participation, and whether the project can continue providing value to the open-source community.

## Approach
- It proposes splitting the project out of Manjaro GmbH & Co KG and reorganizing it as a nonprofit registered association (e.V.), equally owned and governed by existing team members who choose to join.
- It adopts a flat/decentralized structure: members may take on responsibilities based on interest, while each area has arbiters responsible for direction and approvals, avoiding situations where no one is responsible or where people act beyond their authority.
- It establishes explicit mechanisms for member admission, departure, suspension, and removal, including endorsement, voting, and rules for prolonged loss of contact.
- It establishes a formal voting system: ordinary matters pass by simple majority, while high-impact matters require support from more than 70% of members; the standard voting period is 2 weeks, high-impact matters get 4 weeks, with possible extensions.
- It defines a new relationship between the company and the community project: the company becomes downstream, and the project prioritizes serving the community; at the same time, key assets such as trademark licensing, infrastructure, domain names, code repositories, the forum, CDN, and finances are transferred to the new entity.

## Results
- The document **does not provide experimental, benchmark, or technical performance quantitative results**, because it is fundamentally a governance proposal rather than technical research.
- The proposal gives several explicit institutional parameters: members may be removed after being unreachable for **12 months**; voluntary suspension can extend that period to **36 months**; ordinary voting lasts **2 weeks**, and high-impact voting lasts **4 weeks**.
- High-impact decisions require **70%+** support, with **5/7 ≈ 71%** given as a reference threshold for the minimum member size of the e.V.
- Regarding trademarks, it proposes that the company grant the project e.V. an exclusive license to the Manjaro trademark until **the end of 2029**, and transfer the trademark for **1 euro** after the initial period.
- The proposal lists **19 supporters**, indicating that it claims to have gained a certain level of support from team and community representatives.
- The strongest practical claim is that if negotiations fail, actions will proceed in stages, including publishing documents publicly, launching a general strike, and in later stages considering a fork or departure from the existing Manjaro project.
