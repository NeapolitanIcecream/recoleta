# Doomporn

## Summary
This article is not a research paper, but a short commentary on AI discourse. The author argues against the fatalistic “doomporn” narrative, advocating for a rational view of AI amid uncertainty and for incorporating AI tools into real workflows as soon as possible.

## Problem
- The issue discussed in the article is that public discussion around AI’s impact is being dominated by “doomsday-style” narratives, which can amplify panic, distort judgment, and even affect market sentiment.
- This matters because societies, organizations, and individuals all need to decide whether and how to adopt AI based on reality rather than emotion.
- The author emphasizes that no one truly knows AI’s long-term outcome, so a single disaster prophecy cannot provide a reliable basis for action.

## Approach
- The core mechanism is not a technical method, but a **cognitive framework**: using four reminders to correct how we judge AI.
- First, like past major technologies, AI will bring both positive and negative effects; we cannot look at only one side.
- Second, no one knows the future, and most current views are essentially guesses and bets.
- Third, many people participating in the discussion are “talking their book,” meaning their views are often driven by their own economic interests and positions.
- Fourth, when facing change, the most practical response is not to indulge in “doom” narratives, but to actively learn and integrate AI tools into workflows.

## Results
- No experiments, datasets, metrics, or quantitative results are provided; this is not an empirical research paper.
- The strongest specific claim is that AI’s impact will include both positive and negative sides, and that these negative effects will roughly accompany its positive value, but the article provides no numerical evidence.
- The author claims that “no one knows the future,” so current predictions of AI doom or prosperity should be treated as highly uncertain bets rather than firm conclusions.
- The practical takeaway offered is: if you are in the workforce, you should begin using AI tools and incorporate them into your workflow; however, the article does not report productivity gains or baseline comparison figures.
