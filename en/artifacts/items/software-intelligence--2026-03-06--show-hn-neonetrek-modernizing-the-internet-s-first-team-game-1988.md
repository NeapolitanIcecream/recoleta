# Show HN: NeoNetrek – modernizing the internet's first team game (1988)

## Summary
NeoNetrek modernizes Netrek, an early 1988 multiplayer team network game, into a real-time team battle experience that runs directly in the browser. Its core value lies in bringing the classic, complex, persistent multiplayer cooperative gameplay back to the modern web with zero installation.

## Problem
- Early internet multiplayer team games typically relied on native clients, complex installation, and legacy runtime environments, creating a high barrier to entry for new players.
- Classic team-based competitive gameplay and persistent-world experiences are difficult to access seamlessly on the modern web, limiting the spread and revival of historical games.
- For multiplayer games that require real-time coordination and low-friction access, cross-platform instant play is important because it directly affects player acquisition and retention.

## Approach
- Reimplement classic Netrek as a modern web version so that the game **runs entirely in the browser**, using **WebSocket** for real-time networked interaction.
- Preserve the original core mechanics: a persistent galaxy, team coordination, starship control, planetary conquest, and four-faction warfare.
- Offer 16-player team battles and multiple ship roles, from Scout to Starbase, each with different abilities and tactical roles.
- Reuse and modernize the classic combat systems, including torpedoes, phasers, tractors, cloaking, and plasma weapons, to maintain the original depth.
- Use "no downloads, no plugins, no installs, just open a URL and play" as the primary product mechanism to reduce adoption friction.
