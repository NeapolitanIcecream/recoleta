# Ask HN: Anyone else feel this community has changed recently?

## Summary
This is not a research paper, but a Hacker News community discussion post whose core focus is reflecting on changes in HN's content and atmosphere in recent years. The author believes that the flood of AI content, the decline in startup projects, and increasingly politicized discussion have reduced the community's appeal.

## Problem
- The “problem” under discussion is whether the Hacker News community has changed noticeably in recent years, especially whether content quality and user experience have declined.
- The author considers this important because HN used to be seen as a platform for high-quality technical discussion and independent project launches; if the community culture is now shifting, it affects how technical people get information, communicate, and discover new projects.
- The text also implies a broader issue: whether technical communities are being reshaped by AI topics dominated by big companies, commercialization, and a less tolerant opinion environment.

## Approach
- This piece does not use a formal research method; instead, it relies on a **long-term user's personal observations and subjective comparison**, comparing the HN experience since 2010 with the past few years.
- The author summarizes the changes into three mechanisms: a **rise in the share of AI posts**, a **decline in independent startups/project launches**, and a **more politicized comments section that is less tolerant of minority views**.
- To explain why AI content feels “less technical,” the author gives a contrasting example: a subjective comparison of AI models drawing pelicans is less interesting than a concrete engineering optimization article like “cutting GTA Online loading times by 70%.”
- The author also suggests a deeper explanation: in the past, tech products were more often “small teams creating new experiences for users,” whereas current LLMs are more like “automation tools built by big companies with massive investment for commercial goals.”

## Results
- There are no quantitative experiments, datasets, baselines, or statistical tests, so there are **no paper-style quantitative results**.
- The only explicit numerical information in the text is that the author says they have used HN under different aliases since **2010**, and believes the negative changes mainly happened in the past **few years**.
- The most specific engineering comparison mentioned is the external article's claim of **cutting GTA Online loading times by 70%**; however, this is only used to illustrate the author's preferred type of content and is **not a result of this post itself**.
- The strongest concrete claims are that **AI-related posts have increased significantly** on HN, **independent startups/new project launches have become less common**, and the **comments section is more prone to one-sidedness, downvoting, and flagging**.
- The concluding claim is that these changes made the author “seriously consider for the first time whether to continue using HN,” indicating a significant decline in their subjective satisfaction.
