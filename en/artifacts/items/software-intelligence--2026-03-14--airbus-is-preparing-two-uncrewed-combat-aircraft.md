# Airbus is preparing two uncrewed combat aircraft

## Summary
This is an industry/defense project announcement rather than a strict academic paper. It describes Airbus integrating the Kratos Valkyrie with its European sovereign mission system, MARS, with the goal of providing the German Air Force an operational Uncrewed Collaborative Combat Aircraft system by 2029.

## Problem
- The problem to solve is: how to provide Germany/Europe with operationally meaningful uncrewed collaborative combat capability in a **short time and at an affordable cost**, rather than developing an entire platform from scratch.
- This matters because the current geopolitical environment requires combat capability to be fielded more quickly, while still ensuring a **European sovereign mission system and control authority**.
- It also aims to address command in manned-unmanned teaming, cross-platform connectivity, and substitution in high-risk missions, so that uncrewed aircraft can take on tasks that are too dangerous for pilots.

## Approach
- The core method is simple: directly use the **proven, in-production** Kratos Valkyrie flight platform, then integrate Airbus’s European sovereign mission system **MARS** to accelerate delivery.
- MARS includes an AI-supported software “brain,” **MindShare**, used to replace part of the decision-making and control functions of the absent pilot, and to coordinate entire mission formations in a distributed manner across multiple manned and unmanned platforms.
- The system supports multiple operating modes: fully autonomous execution, command by Eurofighter, or manned-unmanned teaming (MUM-T).
- To enable Eurofighter to serve as the “command aircraft,” Airbus and Rafael are enhancing the connectivity of the **Litening 5** pod and making minor upgrades to Eurofighter avionics to improve cross-platform coordination and combat effectiveness.

## Results
- In terms of timeline, Airbus is preparing **2** Valkyries, with a first flight carrying the European sovereign mission system planned for **later in 2026**; the goal is to provide the German Air Force with an operational UCCA system by **2029**.
- In terms of baseline platform performance, the Valkyrie is **9.1 meters** long, has a wingspan of **8.2 meters**, a range of **more than 5,000 kilometers**, a maximum takeoff weight of about **3 tons**, and a service ceiling of **45,000 feet**.
- Regarding reliability claims, the original Valkyrie completed its first flight in the United States in **2019**, and additional aircraft have continued flying since then; the text uses this as the basis for calling it “flight-proven” and “in-production.”
- Regarding capability claims, the system is described as being able to operate independently, operate collaboratively as an uncrewed swarm, or participate in manned-unmanned teaming, while supporting kinetic and non-kinetic missions.
- The text **does not provide standard academic quantitative metrics** (such as accuracy, mission success rate, baseline comparison experiments, ablation studies, etc.), so there is no verifiable research-performance breakthrough data; the strongest specific claim is that integrating an off-the-shelf platform with a sovereign mission system enables combat capability that is faster, cheaper, and more scalable to deploy.
