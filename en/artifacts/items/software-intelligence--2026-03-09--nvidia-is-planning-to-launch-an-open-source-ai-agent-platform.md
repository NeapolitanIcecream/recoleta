# Nvidia Is Planning to Launch an Open-Source AI Agent Platform

## Summary
This is a news report about Nvidia’s plan to launch an open-source enterprise AI agent platform called “NemoClaw,” rather than a formal academic paper. Its core value lies in bringing safer, more controllable AI agent capabilities to the enterprise software ecosystem, while using that to expand Nvidia’s software influence beyond AI infrastructure.

## Problem
- Although existing chatbots are more reliable, they still typically require substantial human guidance and struggle to consistently complete multi-step work tasks.
- Enterprises face clear risks when using autonomous AI agents/claws, including unpredictable behavior, privacy leaks, and security issues, which limits real-world adoption.
- Amid intensifying competition and increasing customer development of in-house chips, Nvidia needs to find a software platform lever that goes beyond CUDA lock-in.

## Approach
- Nvidia plans to launch an open-source AI agent platform called **NemoClaw**, allowing enterprise software companies to dispatch agents to perform tasks for their employees.
- The platform is reportedly designed to **not require running on Nvidia chips**, lowering adoption barriers and expanding ecosystem reach.
- Nvidia is in contact with companies such as Salesforce, Cisco, Google, Adobe, and CrowdStrike, seeking to build a partner ecosystem around the platform.
- To address enterprise concerns, the platform plans to provide **security and privacy tools** to improve the controllability of agents in enterprise environments.
- The platform also continues Nvidia’s recent open-source AI strategy, using an open platform to attract developers and enterprises rather than relying solely on the proprietary CUDA system.

## Results
- The report **does not provide any quantitative experimental results**, benchmark tests, dataset performance, or numerical comparisons with competing products.
- The most concrete conclusion is that Nvidia **plans** to release an open-source agent platform and has already pitched it to multiple large enterprise software companies.
- The report claims the platform will support enterprises in dispatching AI agents to execute work tasks and will include security/privacy capabilities, but **does not provide figures for performance, success rate, cost, or reliability**.
- The article only provides industry-background examples: Meta reportedly restricted employee use of certain agent tools due to risk, and a security executive publicly mentioned an incident in which an agent went out of control and deleted emails in bulk; these are examples of risk, not result data for NemoClaw.
