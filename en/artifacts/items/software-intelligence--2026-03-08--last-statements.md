# Last Statements

## Summary

## Problem
- This text does not attempt to solve a technical problem in software, AI, or engineering.

## Approach
- The core mechanism is very simple: it compiles **last statements** from Texas death row inmates case by case, paired with brief background descriptions of the corresponding crimes.
- Each record typically contains two parts: a verbatim final statement and a summary of the crime facts and victim information.
- In terms of content, this is more like a **manually curated data archive / corpus** than a research work with models, algorithms, or experimental design.
- It could be used in subsequent research for thematic analysis of expression patterns such as remorse, religious language, denial of guilt, messages to family, and criticism of the system.

## Results

- The examples show several recurring concrete themes in the statements, including apologizing to victims’ families, religious / Christian language, thanking family and supporters, claiming repentance or “change,” and in some cases maintaining innocence.
