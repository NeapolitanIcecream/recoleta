# Turns out making games is the easy part

## Summary
This content is not an academic paper, but rather a product introduction: the author built a free browser gaming platform centered on a retro Flash-era feel, with built-in leaderboards, achievements, and a "champion showcase" mechanism. Its core value proposition is combining player competition with social exposure to help the platform boost engagement and word-of-mouth.

## Problem
- The problem it aims to solve is: for indie game/minigame platforms, it is not enough to simply **make games**—the harder challenge is **getting players to discover them, engage, and keep coming back**.
- This matters because for free web gaming platforms, distribution, retention, and community activity often determine success more than the development of any single game.
- The post also implicitly points out a common pain point: developers spend their time building content but lack effective user acquisition and visibility mechanisms.

## Approach
- Build a **free browser gaming platform** positioned as a collection of minigames with an **old Flash-era feel**.
- The platform currently has **3 games live** and plans to launch a **zombie dungeon looter**, using ongoing releases to expand content supply.
- Integrate **competitive leaderboards** and **achievements** at the platform level, rather than offering only a single-player-style gameplay experience.
- The core mechanism is the **champion system**: the social accounts of the player at the top of the leaderboard are featured on the homepage, using visible exposure rewards to encourage competition and sharing.
- The method is fundamentally very simple: replace purely virtual rewards with **top-rank prestige + social visibility**, motivating players to engage more deeply and become more willing to spread the platform externally.

## Results
- The text **does not provide formal quantitative experimental results**: there is no dataset, evaluation metrics, baseline comparison, or A/B testing data.
- The concrete outputs achieved so far include: **1 free browser gaming platform**, **3 games already launched**, and **1 upcoming new game**.
- The strongest functional claim is that the platform includes **leaderboards, an achievement system, and a champion showcase mechanism**, in which the champion receives **homepage social exposure**.
- The author says the innovation they are "most proud of" is the **champion system**, and explicitly notes they are **not sure whether it has been done before**. As such, the novelty is more of a product design experiment than a validated technical breakthrough.
- In terms of impact, the text only states the expected value: using competition and player spotlight to improve feedback, sharing, and engagement, but it **does not provide numbers for user growth, retention, activity, or conversion rate**.
