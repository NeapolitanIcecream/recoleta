# Artificial Intelligence as a Catalyst for Innovation in Software Engineering

## Summary
This paper explores how AI acts as a "catalyst" in software engineering to enhance agile development, automation, and innovation, combining a literature review with an exploratory survey of practitioners for analysis. The overall conclusion is that respondents generally view AI as a software engineering support technology that can simultaneously improve productivity, agility, and innovation.

## Problem
- Software engineering faces long-standing challenges such as frequently changing requirements, tight delivery cycles, and difficulty ensuring quality, while traditional rigid processes struggle to adapt sustainably.
- Even when using agile methods, teams still encounter bottlenecks in requirements management, cross-team collaboration, large-scale project coordination, and quality control.
- The paper focuses on whether and how AI can enhance agility and promote innovation across the full software engineering lifecycle, which is important for improving development efficiency and competitiveness.

## Approach
- It adopts a mixed research approach of **literature review + empirical survey**, examining the role of AI in requirements engineering, design and planning, coding, testing, operations, and project management.
- The survey was distributed to people in both software engineering academia and industry, with more than **1,000** email invitations sent and **64** complete responses received to explore AI adoption, perceived impact, and future expectations.
- Descriptive statistics were used to analyze closed-ended questions, and thematic coding was applied to open-ended responses to summarize perceived AI impacts on agility, productivity, and innovation.
- To validate the scale structure, exploratory factor analysis (EFA) and reliability analysis were conducted; the results showed that the relevant items could be explained by a single latent factor, namely "perceived impact and adoption readiness of AI in software engineering."

## Results
- The survey data were suitable for factor analysis: **KMO = 0.870**, Bartlett's test of sphericity **χ² = 197.802, df = 21, p < .001**.
- The EFA yielded a **single-factor structure**, with model fit **χ² = 18.688, df = 14, p = 0.177**, supporting the treatment of AI impact as a holistic perceived construct.
- Item factor loadings ranged from **0.58–0.86**; the highest loadings included innovation **Q10 = 0.855**, productivity **Q8 = 0.800**, and agility **Q6 = 0.774**, indicating that respondents valued these AI benefits most.
- The scale showed strong internal consistency: **McDonald’s ω = 0.882** (95% CI **0.837–0.926**), **Cronbach’s α = 0.872** (95% CI **0.807–0.937**).
- Item-total correlations ranged from **0.538–0.791**, indicating that all items maintained positive and substantial relationships with the common construct.
- The paper also cites related studies claiming that AI can improve the accuracy of automated code review and defect detection to **as high as 95%**; however, this is not a primary result directly measured by this paper’s survey experiment, but rather external evidence from the literature review.
