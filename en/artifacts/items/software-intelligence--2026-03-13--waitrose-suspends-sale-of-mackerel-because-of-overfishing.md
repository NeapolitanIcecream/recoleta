# Waitrose suspends sale of mackerel because of overfishing

## Summary
This article reports that the UK supermarket Waitrose has suspended sales of mackerel due to overfishing and is instead directing consumers toward more sustainable alternative species. Its core significance lies in using retail-side intervention to respond to the ecological risk of fishery resources nearing collapse.

## Problem
- The issue highlighted in the article is that **north-east Atlantic mackerel stocks are nearing unsustainable levels and even potential collapse due to long-term overfishing**, with implications for marine ecosystems and the long-term supply of fisheries.
- This matters because mackerel is a commonly consumed fish, but current catch levels are already significantly above scientific recommendations; without adjustment, this could lead to stock collapse and affect other wildlife species.
- Existing governance is inadequate: countries’ catch-reduction commitments fall short of scientific recommendations, and responsibility cannot rest solely with retailers and consumers.

## Approach
- The core mechanism is simple: **Waitrose suspends sales of mackerel** to reduce support from the demand side of the market for overfished species.
- At the same time, the supermarket will **steer consumers toward more sustainable alternatives**, such as herring, sardines, and sea bass.
- This approach is based on signals from external scientific and environmental bodies, including the Marine Conservation Society’s downgrade, ICES catch-reduction recommendations, and academic advice to broaden consumption toward sustainable fish species.
- The article also emphasizes the need for supporting policy measures: governments should set catch limits that better reflect scientific evidence rather than relying only on voluntary market adjustment.

## Results
- Waitrose said it will **stop sourcing fresh, chilled, and frozen mackerel by April 29**, while canned mackerel will be withdrawn once existing stock is sold out.
- The Marine Conservation Society had already **downgraded mackerel from level 3 to level 4 on its 5-point sustainability rating**, indicating worsening sustainability.
- In September last year, ICES recommended that north-east Atlantic mackerel catches be **reduced by 70%** to help the population recover to sustainable levels.
- But in December, the relevant nations of the UK agreed only to a **48% reduction**, clearly below the ICES recommendation.
- WWF said that since **2010**, mackerel catches in the region have averaged **39% above recommended levels**.
- The article does not provide academic performance metrics for any “method” on an experimental dataset; the strongest concrete result is that Waitrose became the **first UK supermarket to suspend mackerel sales**, directly responding to the overfishing crisis through retail policy.
