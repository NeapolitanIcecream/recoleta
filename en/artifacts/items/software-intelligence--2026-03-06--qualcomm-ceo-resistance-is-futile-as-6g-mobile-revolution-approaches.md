# Qualcomm CEO: 'Resistance Is Futile' as 6G Mobile Revolution Approaches

## Summary
This article is not an academic paper, but an industry report on Qualcomm’s vision for 6G. The core argument is that 6G will become a “native wireless network” for the AI era, supporting large-scale real-time collaboration among devices, the edge, the cloud, and AI agents.

## Problem
- Existing mobile networks are mainly designed for **human-driven voice, video, and app traffic**, and are not well suited to supporting the continuous communication among large numbers of **AI agents** in the future.
- If interaction shifts from an “app economy” to an “agent economy,” networks must simultaneously meet higher demands for **reliability, low latency, bandwidth efficiency**, and cross-device coordination.
- This matters because it will determine whether phones, glasses, cars, PCs, and wearables can become unified entry points for AI computing and services, rather than remaining isolated terminals.

## Approach
- Qualcomm proposes designing 6G as an **AI-native wireless network**, meaning deeply integrating **connectivity capabilities and AI capabilities at the network layer**.
- The core mechanism can be understood simply as enabling **on-device computing, edge computing, cloud computing, and wireless networks** to work together to support real-time “conversations” among AI agents across different devices and services.
- The components emphasized in the article include: **on-device capabilities**, **edge computing**, **AI-enabled load control**, and stronger cloud access capabilities.
- On the user side, the goal is to shift from “opening multiple apps to complete multiple tasks” to “interacting with an AI agent that knows you and invokes various services on your behalf.”

## Results
- The article **does not provide rigorous experiments, datasets, metrics, or paper-style baseline comparisons**, so there is no verifiable quantitative breakthrough.
- The strongest specific claim is that Qualcomm believes **the first consumer test applications of 6G will appear around the 2028 Los Angeles Olympics**.
- Another explicit timeline claim is: **rollout will begin in 2029**.
- Qualitatively, the article claims that 6G will, for the first time, **combine connectivity and AI within the network**, and shift network traffic from being primarily driven by consumer content consumption to being dominated by **AI agent traffic**.
- Example application visions include: **real-time information prompts in AR glasses, the car as a computing interface, a unified agent replacing multiple apps, and automated scenarios that coordinate shopping/travel services in advance**.
