# Lenovo’s new ThinkPads score 10/10 for repairability

## Summary
This article explains that Lenovo’s new ThinkPad T14 Gen 7 and T16 Gen 5 have, for the first time, earned **10/10** in iFixit’s repairability scoring, marking a higher standard for “repairability” in mainstream business laptops. The significance is that repairability is no longer just a niche selling point, but is starting to become a core metric in large-scale enterprise procurement and device lifecycle management.

## Problem
- Laptops usually involve tradeoffs among performance, thickness, thermal design, and structural integrity, making repairs “theoretically possible” but inconvenient in practice, which raises enterprise device downtime and maintenance costs.
- Traditional serviceability design is often considered only late in development, when many structural and layout decisions are already locked in, making truly high-level repairability difficult to achieve.
- For repair to become mainstream, it must be realized in high-volume, mainstream business models, rather than remaining limited to low-volume concept products or enthusiast devices.

## Approach
- Lenovo moved repairability earlier in the design process, having design, engineering, service, quality, and sustainability teams make coordinated decisions from the very beginning of the project.
- The core mechanism is simple: make high-failure-rate or frequently replaced components **easier to access, independently replaceable, and as modular as possible**.
- Specific improvements include: near tool-free battery replacement, use of standard M.2 SSDs, very easy keyboard replacement, serviceable LPCAMM2 memory, simplified display repair, a modular thermal system with independently replaceable fans, and fully modular Thunderbolt ports.
- Lenovo and iFixit repeatedly disassembled, evaluated, provided feedback, and iterated during development to fix design details at the “last 0.1 point” level.

## Results
- The new **ThinkPad T14 Gen 7** and **T16 Gen 5** received iFixit **10/10** repairability scores; this is the **first time the T series** has achieved a perfect score, and it is currently a **provisional rating**, pending final confirmation after official parts and repair documentation go live.
- Compared with the previous generation: the repairability-focused T14 generation from two years ago scored **9/10**, and the new generation improved from **9/10 to 10/10**.
- The article’s central breakthrough claim is achieving perfect repairability in the high-volume category of **mainstream business laptops**, rather than experimenting on niche models.
- The article does not provide benchmark data in the traditional academic sense, such as failure rates, repair times, or percentage cost reductions, but the strongest concrete evidence given is that several key components now support convenient or modular replacement: the battery, SSD, keyboard, memory, fan, Thunderbolt ports, and display assembly.
- The article also makes clear that it is still not absolutely perfect: not all I/O is fully modular, Wi‑Fi module upgrades/repair are still less than ideal, and deep repair of the display panel/camera still involves some adhesive structures.
