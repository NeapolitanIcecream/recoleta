# Atlassian cuts another 1,600 jobs amid AI shakeup

## Summary
This is not a research paper, but a business news report on Atlassian laying off staff due to an AI transformation and enterprise business restructuring. Its core message is that the company is cutting about 1,600 roles to free up resources for investment in AI and enterprise sales.

## Problem
- The issue discussed is not a technical challenge, but how a company adjusts its organizational structure, skill requirements, and cost structure under the impact of AI.
- Its importance lies in the fact that AI is changing the composition of jobs and investment priorities at software companies, affecting employees, management, and capital market judgment.
- It has limited direct value for the research field, because the content does not involve models, algorithms, datasets, or experimental design.

## Approach
- Atlassian’s “approach” is business restructuring: cutting about 1,600 employees, roughly 10% of its global workforce.
- The company explicitly states that this move is intended to “self-fund” continued investment in AI and enterprise sales, while also improving its financial position.
- The CEO pointed out that AI is changing the mix of skills the company needs and reducing demand for certain functional roles.
- The restructuring also includes reorganizing teams around its “system of work” to increase execution speed.

## Results
- The clearest result is the scale of the organizational adjustment: **1,600 employees** laid off, about **10%** of the workforce.
- Affected employees will receive at least **16 weeks** of separation pay, **6 months** of extended healthcare, and a prorated **FY26 bonus**.
- The article states that the company’s share price has fallen about **64%** over the past **12 months**, a key financial pressure signal in the background to the restructuring.
- CTO **Rajeev Rajan** is specifically named as one of the departing executives, indicating that the adjustment affects senior leadership and the technical organization.
- No quantitative technical results for any AI system are provided, such as model metrics, dataset performance, baseline comparisons, or experimental data.
