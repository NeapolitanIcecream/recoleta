# Civic opinion sharing to promote democracy

## Summary
OurPublicVoice is a nonpartisan civic engagement platform that lets the public read summaries of policy issues, cast support, oppose, or neutral votes, and then view real-time anonymous comparisons from the community level up to the national level. It attempts to narrow the gap between public sentiment and legislative action through a more transparent, data-driven way of aggregating opinions.

## Problem
- The problem the platform aims to solve is that public opinion on policy issues is often difficult to systematically collect, visualize, and convey into the public decision-making process.
- This matters because democratic governance depends on broad, comparable, and visible public voices; without such a mechanism, legislation and public opinion can easily become disconnected.
- The text also suggests another pain point: citizens often lack simple tools to filter issues by local relevance and understand how their own views differ from those of their community/state/nation.

## Approach
- The core mechanism is simple: first provide users with **nonpartisan policy summaries**, then let them express one of three positions on each issue: **support / oppose / neutral**.
- The platform aggregates voting results into **real-time, anonymized demographic breakdowns**, helping users compare opinion distributions in their community, state, and the nation.
- Issues span multiple levels — **federal, state, county, city** — and support geographic filtering, so users can prioritize policy matters that directly affect them.
- Users can create accounts to keep voting on and tracking issues, thereby forming a continuously updated layer of civic opinion data.

## Results
- The provided text **does not give quantitative experimental results, user scale, accuracy, participation rates, or baseline comparisons with other platforms**.
