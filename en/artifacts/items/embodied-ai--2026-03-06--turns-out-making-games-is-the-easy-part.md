# Turns out making games is the easy part

## Summary
This is not a research paper, but a product introduction for a free browser gaming platform. Its core selling points are a retro Flash-style experience, built-in leaderboards and achievements, and a "champion system" that lets the top player display their social accounts on the homepage.

## Problem
- The problem it aims to solve is: an indie developer has built the games and the platform, but "doesn't know how to actually show it to users," meaning product distribution and player acquisition are difficult.
- The platform also tries to address the lack of sustained competitive motivation in lightweight web games by using leaderboards, achievements, and visibility incentives to improve retention and engagement.
- This matters because even if game development is complete, without players discovering, sharing, and continuing to engage with the platform, it is hard to build a community and achieve growth.

## Approach
- Build a **free browser gaming platform** centered on the lightweight, easy-to-pick-up feel of the "old Flash era."
- The platform currently has **3 games live**, with the next title announced as **zombie dungeon looter**.
- At the platform level, it has built-in **competitive leaderboards** and **achievements**, making competition and progression core stickiness mechanisms.
- Introduce a **champion system**: the social accounts of the player ranked first on the leaderboard can be featured on the homepage, using public visibility to motivate competition and sharing.
