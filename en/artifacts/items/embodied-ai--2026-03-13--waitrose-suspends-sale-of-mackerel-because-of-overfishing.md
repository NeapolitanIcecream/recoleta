# Waitrose suspends sale of mackerel because of overfishing

## Summary
This article reports that the UK supermarket Waitrose has suspended sales of mackerel because of overfishing, attempting to promote more sustainable seafood consumption through retail-side action. Its core significance lies in using a market signal to respond to declining fish stocks and guide consumers toward more sustainable alternative species.

## Problem
- The issue highlighted is that the northeast Atlantic mackerel population is nearing an unsustainable state and may even collapse due to long-term overfishing, with implications for marine ecosystems, the long-term supply of fisheries, and the safety of related wildlife.
- Existing management measures are insufficient: ICES recommended a **70%** cut in catches, but the UK jurisdictions agreed to only a **48%** reduction, showing that the policy response is weaker than the scientific recommendation.
- Since **2010**, mackerel catches have averaged **39%** above recommended levels, indicating that overfishing is a persistent systemic problem rather than just a short-term fluctuation.

## Approach
- The core action is straightforward: Waitrose suspended procurement and sales of fresh, chilled, and frozen mackerel, and will stop selling canned mackerel once existing stock is sold out.
- At the same time, the supermarket will steer consumers toward more sustainable alternative fish species, such as **herring、sardines、sea bass**, using substitution in consumption to reduce demand pressure on mackerel.
- This mechanism is not a technical method but a supply-chain and retail policy intervention: by removing high-risk species from shelves, it sends a sustainability signal both upstream to fisheries and downstream to consumers.
- The article also cites academic and environmental organizations, emphasizing that the entire supply chain and government need to keep fishing within sustainable limits, and that consumer choice alone is not enough.

## Results
- The clearest concrete result is that Waitrose became the **first supermarket in the UK** to suspend sales of mackerel because of overfishing.
- In **September** last year, ICES recommended cutting northeast Atlantic mackerel catches by **70%** to help the population recover to a sustainable level.
- In **December**, the UK jurisdictions agreed to only a **48%** reduction, clearly below the scientifically recommended **70%**.
- WWF said that since **2010**, relevant catches have exceeded recommended levels by an average of **39%**.
- MCS had already downgraded mackerel in the five-level Good Fish Guide sustainability rating from **3** to **4**, indicating worsening sustainability.
- The article does not provide model metrics or causal evaluation results like an experimental paper would; the strongest concrete claim is that a retail sales suspension and recommendations for alternatives can serve as a direct market measure to address overfishing and protect mackerel stocks and marine ecosystems.
