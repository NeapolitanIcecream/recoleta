# Manjaro Linux 2.0 Manifesto

## Summary
This is not a research paper, but a **Manjaro Linux 2.0** project governance manifesto that advocates rescuing an open-source distribution project in decline due to centralized leadership, loss of trust, and poor infrastructure management through organizational restructuring.

## Problem
- The document aims to address **organizational governance failure in the Manjaro project**: contributor attrition, declining community trust, and known operations issues remaining unfixed for long periods.
- Its importance lies in the claim that the project’s technical quality, use of funds, control over infrastructure, and community sustainability are all being hindered by the current centralized governance model.
- The text explicitly states that without reform, the project may continue to decline and could even face a strike, a fork, or a team walkout.

## Approach
- The core mechanism is to **separate** the project from the corporate entity into a **nonprofit registered association (e.V.)**, so the project becomes community-led again rather than dominated by a single individual.
- It adopts **flat/decentralized governance**: existing willing members have equal ownership, while different areas are guided and approved by arbiters.
- It operates through a **nomination + voting** mechanism for membership and decisions: new members must receive endorsements from more than 2 people; important matters require a vote, and higher-impact matters require more than **70%** approval.
- It redefines the relationship with the company: the project entity serves the community first, while the company becomes downstream; the trademark would be licensed through 2029, with an intention to transfer it afterward for 1 euro.
- It proposes transferring key **assets and infrastructure** (GitHub/GitLab, domains, forums, CDN, cloud resources, financial channels, etc.) to the new community entity, while allowing the company to continue using some resources on a cost-reimbursement basis.

## Results
- The text **does not provide quantitative research results in the sense of experiments, datasets, or benchmarks**, so there are no comparable model performance metrics.
- Specific institutional figures include: high-impact proposals require **>70%** member approval; the standard voting period is **2 weeks**, the high-impact voting period is **4 weeks**, and it may be extended by up to **4 additional weeks**.
- The condition for removing team members is set at at least **12 months** without contact; if a member self-suspends, the period is extended to **36 months**.
- The trademark arrangement proposes that the company grant the new entity an exclusive trademark license until the **end of 2029**, after which the trademark could be transferred for **1 euro**.
- The strongest practical claim is that through nonprofit status, decentralized decision-making, and infrastructure transfer, Manjaro could restore its reputation, attract contributors, and rebuild community value, but these remain governance proposals rather than verified outcomes.
