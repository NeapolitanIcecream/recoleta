# Ask HN: Most beautiful personal blog UI you have ever seen?

## Summary
This is not a research paper, but rather a Hacker News discussion thread on the topic "What is the most beautiful personal blog UI you have ever seen?" The content mainly consists of users sharing examples of excellent blog design and discussing the trade-offs among aesthetics, readability, and usability.

## Problem
- The question the discussion seeks to answer is: what kind of personal blog UI is considered "the most beautiful," and whether that kind of "beauty" also comes with informativeness and a good user experience.
- This matters because blog design affects not only first impressions, but also content consumption efficiency, navigation experience, and readability.
- The thread also implicitly raises a practical question: whether blogs that have appeared on HN could be automatically categorized into S-tier, A-tier, opinion, and so on based on subjective criteria.

## Approach
- The main mechanism is not experimental research, but rather **community-crowdsourced subjective evaluation**: users post links to blogs they consider excellent and classify them by style, such as artsy, simple, typography, old-school, and maximalist.
- The original poster proposes a simple filtering criterion: a preference for blogs with "high information density" that let readers learn something, and a dislike of purely opinion-based blogs.
- Commenters further add evaluation dimensions, which at their core compare three of the simplest factors: **visual impact, layout/typography, and navigation and interaction UX**.
- The discussion also points out several common failure modes: scroll hijacking, back button/history hijacking, headings that look like links but are not, overly long click paths to reach subpages, and unclear loading states.

## Results
- No quantitative experiments, datasets, metrics, or baseline comparisons are provided, so there are **no paper-style breakthrough results to report**.
- The strongest concrete conclusion is that several repeatedly mentioned sites are regarded as "top-tier examples," including `ciechanow.ski`, `lumafield.com`, `growingswe.com`, `mlu-explain.github.io`, `seeing-theory.brown.edu`, and `svg-tutorial.com`.
- The only statement in the thread that comes close to quantification is a negative UX example: one site requires **3 clicks and 10 seconds** to reach a subpage, which is seen as significantly harming the first experience.
- The consensus that emerges from the discussion is that **a beautiful blog UI is not just about visual design, but must also balance readability, clear navigation, and restrained interaction**; excessive showiness weakens the overall experience.
- Another clear claim is that even if the design is very basic, users will still highly appreciate it as long as the content is excellent, the layout is clear, and whitespace and proportions are handled well.
