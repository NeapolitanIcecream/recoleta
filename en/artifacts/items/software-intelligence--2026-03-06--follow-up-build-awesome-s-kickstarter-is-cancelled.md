# Follow-Up: Build Awesome's Kickstarter Is Cancelled

## Summary
This is a commentary article about Build Awesome's Kickstarter being canceled, not a technical research paper. The author believes the official reason for canceling the crowdfunding is suspicious, and further criticizes the commercialization and community governance direction of Build Awesome/11ty.

## Problem
- The issue discussed in the article is that Build Awesome's Kickstarter was canceled even after reaching its goal within one day; the official explanation was that launch emails only reached **5-10%** of the target audience, causing the project to "lose momentum."
- The author argues that the more important issue is not just the crowdfunding cancellation, but that the **business model and community decision-making approach of 11ty/Build Awesome** may harm the sustainability of the open-source project and community trust.
- This matters because static site generators like 11ty are not just tools, but also open-source infrastructure that depends on community, ecosystem, and maintainer governance.

## Approach
- This is not an experimental paper; the core mechanism is **opinion-based argumentation and case comparison**, using the Kickstarter incident, community reactions, and industry examples to support the author's judgment.
- The author reduces the issue to this: **even if the product itself is not necessarily the problem, using "collaborative visual editing + in-browser building + paid templates/hosted import tools" to monetize a static site generator is not viable.**
- To support this point, the author cites **GatsbyJS, StackBit, NetlifyCMS** as negative examples, and proposes more sustainable alternative models, such as mentioning **Vike**'s approach of charging larger teams.
- The author also argues from a community perspective that 11ty's unique value is not just its technical features, but its community, ecosystem, and identity; rebranding without community input would weaken those assets.

## Results
- The only clearly numeric fact in the article is that the Kickstarter **reached its funding goal within 1 day**, but was still canceled afterward; the official reason was that launch emails reached only **5-10%** of the intended audience.
- No system experiments, benchmarks, datasets, or quantitative performance metrics are provided, so there are **no reproducible technical results**.
- The strongest concrete claim is that the author believes the following business model is **not viable** for static site generators: **collaborative visual editing, in-browser site building, paid built-in templates, and hosted import tools**.
- The author further draws a strong conclusion: Font Awesome's rebranding and commercialization path for 11ty may mean that **"Eleventy's community identity is ending,"** but this is an opinion rather than empirical evidence.
- The article also offers alternative suggestions: developers should try and contribute to smaller projects, listing **Pelican, Zola, bashblog, Mastro, Web Origami, Kiki, Nanoc** as alternatives.
